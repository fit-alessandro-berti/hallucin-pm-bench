The LLM answer attaches the timer to **Task_RequestDocs** instead of **Task_Receive**, so the 48‑hour clock does not start at submission but only when the process already waits for missing documents. This leaves time spent in *Receive Claim* and *Check Completeness* unmonitored and fails to guarantee the claim reaches triage within 48 hours of submission. The ground truth requires a direct flow from the timer to existing **Task_Triage** so that the claim **reaches** Triage upon timeout, fulfilling both the reach‑Triage requirement and the auto‑escalation implicitly. The LLM solution ends the process with a new *End_Escalated* event, skipping Triage Claim entirely, which violates the rule that every claim must reach Triage Claim. These are critical deviations that make the proposed improvement ineffective for SLA compliance.
