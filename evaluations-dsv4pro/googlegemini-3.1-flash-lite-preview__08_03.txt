The LLM correctly identifies that T5 can occur before T3 and uses the shortcut T2b, but the firing sequence it provides (T1, T2b, T4, T5) is **not** a valid demonstration of “T5 before T3” because T3 never fires in that sequence. To satisfy “fire before T3” both transitions must eventually fire, with T5 preceding T3. The ground truth specifies the shortest sequence as T1, T2b, T4, T5, T6, T3, which includes the necessary feedback loop to re-enable and fire T3 after T5. Missing T6 and T3 is a significant error, making the LLM’s sequence incomplete and not minimal.
