The LLM answer attaches the timer to Task_RequestDocs, which only starts after Receive and Check tasks, and only on the "missing documents" path. This does not guarantee that a claim reaches Triage within 48 hours of submission if delays occur in the preceding Receive or Check activities (which have no timebound control). The ground truth correctly places the interrupting boundary timer on Task_Receive, covering the entire process from claim arrival and ensuring SLA regardless of any upstream delays. The LLM's assumption that Receive/Check time is negligible is not a valid design guarantee; the proposed solution therefore fails to meet the SLA requirement fully. This is a significant conceptual error that drastically reduces the usefulness of the improvement.
