The LLM answer proposes a non-interrupting boundary timer (`cancelActivity="false"`) instead of the required interrupting timer (`cancelActivity="true"`), fundamentally changing the enforcement mechanism. The SLA mandates that the task *must* complete within 48 hours; an interrupting timer halts the overdue task and routes to escalation, directly enforcing the deadline. A non-interrupting timer merely fires an escalation while allowing the original task to continue, which does not guarantee SLA compliance. This core discrepancy, along with the missing `name` attribute on the boundary event, results in a significant loss of points under strict evaluation.
