**2. Justification:**

The FACT claims that *Validate_Payment* directly follows *Receive_Order* in every case. The log contradicts this in two of the four cases:

| Case | Observed sequence | Does Validate_Payment immediately follow Receive_Order? |
|------|-------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| C001 | Receive_Order (09:12) → Validate_Payment (09:15) → Pack_Items | ✅ Yes |
| C002 | Receive_Order (09:30) → **Pack_Items (09:50)** → Validate_Payment (10:05) | ❌ No — *Pack_Items* intervenes |
| C003 | Receive_Order (09:40) → Validate_Payment (09:44) → Ship_Order | ✅ Yes |
| C004 | Receive_Order (10:05) → **Check_Fraud (10:07)** → Validate_Payment (10:20) | ❌ No — *Check_Fraud* intervenes |

**Counterexamples:**
- **C002:** *Pack_Items* (09:50) occurs between *Receive_Order* (09:30) and *Validate_Payment* (10:05).
- **C004:** *Check_Fraud* (10:07) occurs between *Receive_Order* (10:05) and *Validate_Payment* (10:20).

Since the word **"always"** requires the pattern to hold in all cases, and it fails in C002 and C004, the FACT is **not accurate**. The directly-follows relation *Receive_Order → Validate_Payment* holds in only 2 of 4 cases (50%).
