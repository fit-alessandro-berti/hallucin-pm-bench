**Yes.** A shortest firing sequence is:

\[
\boxed{T1,\ T2b,\ T4,\ T5}
\]

Markings:

1. Initially: \(p0=1\)
2. After \(T1\): \(pa=1,\ pb=1\)
3. After \(T2b\): \(pd=1,\ pb=1\)
4. After \(T4\): \(pd=1,\ pe=1\)
5. \(T5\) is enabled and fires, producing \(pf=1\)

Transition \(T3\) has not fired, because \(T2b\) produces \(pd\) directly from \(pa\). Thus \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\). This sequence is shortest because \(T1\), \(T2b\), and \(T4\) are each necessary to produce the inputs \(pd\) and \(pe\) required by \(T5\).
