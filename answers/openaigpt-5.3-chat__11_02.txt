To: COO, Acme Insurance  
Subject: Q1‑2025 Claims Handling Performance – Key Bottlenecks

Three activities stand out as the weakest performers based on wait times, variability, rework, and SLA breaches.

1. Request_Documents  
This step shows the largest delay in the process, with an average wait time of 150 minutes and throughput dropping from 270 to 190 cases/day. It also has a very high rework rate (22%). The data suggests repeated document requests or incomplete submissions are causing queues.  
Action: Introduce a standardized digital intake checklist that validates required documents before submission to reduce rework and queue buildup.

2. Review_Documents  
This activity has the longest processing time (25 minutes), high variability (stdev 9), and the highest SLA breach rate (12.4%). These metrics indicate inconsistent handling and potential complexity differences across reviewers.  
Action: Implement standardized review guidelines and triage simple vs. complex cases to reduce variability and SLA breaches.

3. Initial_Assessment  
Although throughput is high, the step has a relatively large queue (18-minute wait) and a moderate SLA breach rate (3.2%). This indicates capacity pressure early in the process.  
Action: Add capacity or automate basic triage rules to reduce the queue before assessment.

Addressing these three steps should significantly reduce overall cycle time and downstream congestion.
