No, it is not possible for transition T5 to fire *before* transition T3.

**Proof of Impossibility:**

Transition T5 is enabled if and only if place `p4` contains zero tokens (due to the inhibitor arc).

Transition T3 is the only transition that produces a token in place `p4`. Specifically, the firing rule for T3 is `p2 → p4`.

Therefore, to fire T5, `p4` must be empty. Since T3 is the only way to put a token into `p4`, T5 can only fire if T3 has *not yet fired* (or has fired and its output token has been consumed by another transition, which is not possible in this net as `p4` only has an incoming arc from T3).

Since T3 is the producer of tokens for `p4`, and T5 requires `p4` to be empty, T5 cannot fire before T3 has had an opportunity to fire and deposit tokens into `p4`.

In simpler terms, T3 must run at least once to *create* the condition (tokens in p4) that would *prevent* T5 from firing. Consequently, T5 cannot fire until T3 has *already fired* and its tokens have been removed (which doesn't happen directly as T4 consumes from p3 and p4). However, the core constraint is that T5 *cannot fire if p4 has tokens*, and T3 is the sole source of those tokens.
