Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1,\; T2,\; T5
\]

Markings:

- Initial: \(M_0 = [p0=1]\)
- After \(T1\): \(M_1 = [p1=1,\; p2=1]\)
- After \(T2\): \(M_2 = [p3=1,\; p2=1]\)

At \(M_2\):

- \(T5\) has its normal input satisfied because \(p3=1\)
- its inhibitor condition is satisfied because \(p4=0\)

So \(T5\) is enabled at \(M_2\), even though \(T3\) has not fired yet. Therefore \(T5\) can indeed fire before \(T3\).

So the answer is: **Yes, it is possible.**
