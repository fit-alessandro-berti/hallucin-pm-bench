**Key Insight:** After T1 fires (AND-split), P2 sits idle while the upper branch (T2 → T7 → T4) produces P5. At marking **M4 = {P2, P5}**, both T3 and T5 are enabled—but T5 can fire first.

**Minimal sequence:**

$$T_1 \rightarrow T_2 \rightarrow T_7 \rightarrow T_4 \rightarrow T_5$$

| Step | Firing | Consumed | Produced | Resulting Marking |
|------|--------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | T1 | P0 | P1, P2 | {P1, P2} |
| 2 | T2 | P1 | P3 | {P2, P3} |
| 3 | T7 | P3 | P4 | {P2, P4} |
| 4 | T4 | P4 | P5 | {**P2, P5**} |
| 5 | T5 | P2, P5 | P6 | {P6} |
