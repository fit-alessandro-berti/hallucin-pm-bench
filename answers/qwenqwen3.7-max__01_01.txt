Based on the defined Contoso Airlines flight-refund process, here is the summary of the decision points and the list of required documents.

### **Decision Points**

1. **The High-Value Branch (Explicit Routing Decision)**
   * **Condition:** Is the refund amount **greater than €1 000**?
   * **Outcome:** If yes, the case is routed to **Senior_Auditor_Review**. If no, the case bypasses the auditor review and moves directly toward payment processing.

2. **Validation and Pre-Approval Gates (Pass/Fail Decisions)**
   * While not explicitly named as "branches," the following activities act as mandatory decision gates that dictate whether the process can proceed to payment:
     * **Triage_Agent_Validate_Eligibility:** Decides if the claim is valid based on the flight and weather data.
     * **Finance_Controller_PreApprove:** Decides whether to officially sign off on the claim. This is a strict decision gate, as the process constraints dictate that **Process_Refund_Payment** may only begin after this specific activity has **succeeded**.

*(Note: The legacy "CustomerService_Agent_Decide" activity does not exist in this process and is excluded.)*

***

### **Required Documents (Artefacts)**

To successfully execute and complete the process, the following seven documents are required:

1. **RefundRequestForm** (Submitted by the passenger during the initial claim)
2. **FlightManifest** (Used by the triage agent to validate eligibility)
3. **WeatherReport** (Used by the triage agent to validate eligibility)
4. **EligibilityChecklist** (Used by the finance controller to sign off/pre-approve)
5. **AuditTrail** (Required and included specifically for high-value claims during the Senior_Auditor_Review)
6. **BankTransferAuthorization** (Must be signed and present before the finance team can execute the payment)
7. **RefundConfirmationPDF** (Emailed to the passenger at the final step to confirm the refund)
