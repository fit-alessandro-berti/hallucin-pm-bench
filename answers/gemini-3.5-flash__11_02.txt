**MEMO: Q1-2025 Claims Handling Bottlenecks**

**To:** COO, Acme Insurance  

Our Q1-2025 performance dashboard highlights three critical process bottlenecks:

1. **Request_Documents**  
   * **Issue:** Extreme queueing delay with a **150-minute average wait time** and a highly inefficient **22.0% rework rate**.  
   * **Action:** Deploy automated SMS/email document collection templates to clarify requirements upfront and eliminate queueing backlogs.

2. **Review_Documents**  
   * **Issue:** Severe bottlenecking resulting in the process’s highest **SLA breach rate (12.4%)**, longest **processing time (25 mins)**, and highest **variability (9-minute stdev)**.  
   * **Action:** Introduce standardized evaluation checklists to reduce processing variability and accelerate high-complexity reviews.

3. **Initial_Assessment**  
   * **Issue:** Acts as an early-stage drag on throughput with an **18-minute wait time** and a **3.2% SLA breach rate** (the third-worst across both metrics).  
   * **Action:** Reallocate idle capacity from low-wait downstream activities (e.g., Register_Claim) to expedite triage at initial assessment.

Addressing these specific constraints will immediately stabilize downstream throughput and improve customer satisfaction.
