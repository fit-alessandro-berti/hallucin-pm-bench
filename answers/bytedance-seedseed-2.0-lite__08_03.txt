### Answer: Yes, it is possible.
The shortest valid firing sequence is **[T1, T2b, T4, T5]** (the order of T2b and T4 may be swapped, producing another equally short valid sequence).

---
### Verification of the sequence:
1.  Initial marking: Only `p0` holds a token, so only T1 can fire first. Firing T1 consumes the token from `p0`, and places tokens in `pa` and `pb`.
2.  Firing T2b next consumes the token from `pa`, placing a token directly in `pd` (this shortcut skips the normal path through `pc` and T3 entirely, so T3 never needs to fire to get the required `pd` token).
3.  Firing T4 consumes the token from `pb`, placing a token in `pe`.
4.  Now `pd` and `pe` both hold tokens, enabling T5. Firing T5 occurs before T3 (T3 never fires in this sequence, satisfying the requirement that T5 fires first).

This is the shortest possible sequence, as no fewer than 4 transitions can ever enable T5 from the initial marking.
