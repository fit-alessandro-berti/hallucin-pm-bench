The net begins with a single token in \(p_0\). The only enabled transition is therefore \(T_1\), which produces the concurrent tokens
\[
m_1 = \{\,pa,\,pb\,\}.
\]
From \(m_1\) the shortcut \(T_{2b}\) is enabled and yields
\[
m_2 = \{\,pb,\,pd\,\}.
\]
(The ordinary path \(T_2\) is irrelevant for the shortest sequence that avoids \(T_3\).)  
Transition \(T_4\) is now enabled and produces
\[
m_3 = \{\,pd,\,pe\,\}.
\]
Both preconditions of the join \(T_5\) are satisfied, so \(T_5\) may fire, producing
\[
m_4 = \{\,pf\,\}.
\]
In the complete firing sequence
\[
T_1,\,T_{2b},\,T_4,\,T_5
\]
transition \(T_5\) occurs while \(T_3\) has never been enabled (the place \(pc\) never receives a token). No shorter sequence exists, because \(T_1\) is mandatory, one of \(\{T_{2b},T_3\}\) is required to mark \(pd\), and \(T_4\) is required to mark \(pe\).

Thus it is possible for \(T_5\) to fire before \(T_3\).

Final answer: yes, T1 T2b T4 T5
