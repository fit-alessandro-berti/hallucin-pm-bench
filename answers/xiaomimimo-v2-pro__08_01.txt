### Concrete firing sequence:
1. **T1** fires: consumes the token from p0 and produces tokens in p1 and p2.  
   Marking: p0=0, p1=1, p2=1, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0.
2. **T2** fires: consumes the token from p1 and produces a token in p3.  
   Marking: p0=0, p1=0, p2=1, p3=1, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0.  
   At this marking, T5 is enabled because p3 has a token and p4 has zero tokens (inhibitor condition satisfied).
3. **T5** fires: consumes the token from p3 and produces a token in p6.  
   Marking: p0=0, p1=0, p2=1, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=1.  
   T3 has not yet fired (p2 still has a token, so T3 remains enabled but has not fired).

### Justification:
After the parallel split (T1), both T2 and T3 are enabled. If T2 fires first, it places a token in p3 while p4 remains empty. This satisfies the enabling condition for T5 (token in p3 and no token in p4), allowing T5 to fire before T3. Thus, the sequence T1 → T2 → T5 demonstrates that T5 can indeed fire prior to T3.
