**Answer: Yes.**  
Transition T5 can fire before T3.  
A concrete firing sequence from the initial marking is

```
M0  :  p0=1 , p1=0 , p2=0 , p3=0 , p4=0 , p5=0 , p6=0   (initial)

1.  T1  (p0 → p1+p2)
    M1  :  p0=0 , p1=1 , p2=1 , p3=0 , p4=0 , p5=0 , p6=0

2.  T2  (p1 → p3)
    M2  :  p0=0 , p1=0 , p2=1 , p3=1 , p4=0 , p5=0 , p6=0

3.  T5  (p3 –|p4 → p6)      // inhibitor: p4 must be empty – it is (p4=0)
    M3  :  p0=0 , p1=0 , p2=1 , p3=0 , p4=0 , p5=0 , p6=1
```

At marking M2 the pre‑conditions of T5 are satisfied:

* a token is present in **p3** (produced by T2), and  
* **p4** contains **zero** tokens, so the inhibitor arc of T5 is satisfied.

Hence T5 is enabled and can fire **before** T3 (which would later move the token from p2 to p4).  

Thus the net indeed permits a firing of T5 prior to any firing of T3.
