**Yes**, T5 can fire before T3.

**Concrete firing sequence** (from the initial marking \(p_0=1\), all others \(0\)):  
\[
T1,\;T2,\;T5
\]

**Justification**  
- \(T1\) produces one token each in \(p_1\) and \(p_2\).  
- \(T2\) then moves the token from \(p_1\) to \(p_3\).  
- At the resulting marking \(\{p_2=1,p_3=1\}\) place \(p_4\) is still empty, so the inhibitor arc of \(T5\) is satisfied and \(T5\) is enabled; it therefore fires while \(T3\) has not yet occurred.
