**TO:** COO, Acme Insurance
**FROM:** Senior Process Performance Analyst
**DATE:** [Current Date]
**SUBJECT:** Critical Performance Gaps – Q1 Claims Handling

Executive analysis of the Q1 dashboard reveals three specific activities constraining our claims velocity and quality:

**1. Request Documents**
*   **Diagnosis:** This is the primary system bottleneck. A catastrophic **22% rework rate** reduces daily throughput by nearly 30% (from 270 to 190 cases), causing a severe **150-minute average queue**.
*   **Action:** Immediately audit request templates; the data implies we are requesting incorrect or insufficient data, forcing customers to restart the loop.

**2. Review Documents**
*   **Diagnosis:** This activity exhibits the lowest efficiency and highest failure mode. It has the longest **processing time (25 min)**, highest **variability (std dev 9)**, and consequently the worst **SLA breach rate (12.4%)**.
*   **Action:** Deploy standardized decision-support checklists to reduce execution variance and bring handling times within target.

**3. Initial Assessment**
*   **Diagnosis:** Acting as a secondary bottleneck, this stage holds a disproportionate **18-minute wait time**—significantly higher than adjacent steps—and drives **3.2% of total SLA breaches**.
*   **Action:** Reallocate resources or refine triage logic here to clear the backlog before it compounds into the document collection phase.
