MEMORANDUM
TO: Chief Operating Officer, Orion Credit
FROM: Senior Process-Performance Analyst
SUBJECT: Critical Process Bottlenecks and Remediation

Current cycle data isolates three activities severely underperforming due to excessive queue delays. Legal Approval exceeds its Target_SLA by 300 seconds, driven by a 600-second Avg_Waiting_Time. Risk Review also breaches its Target_SLA by 300 seconds, carrying a 480-second Avg_Waiting_Time. Credit Assessment surpasses its Target_SLA by 60 seconds, with a 200-second Avg_Waiting_Time. In each step, waiting periods dominate the Avg_Throughput_Time, directly causing SLA violations across the recorded Cases. To restore compliance, I recommend the following:
• Reassign dedicated reviewers to Legal Approval to directly absorb the documented 600-second queue delay.
• Implement parallel case routing for Risk Review to eliminate the 480-second pre-assessment hold.
• Automate upstream data handoffs for Credit Assessment to remove the 200-second processing wait.

Executing these steps will compress throughput and align performance with established targets.
