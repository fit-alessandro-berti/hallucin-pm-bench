Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
M_0=(1,0,0,0,0,0,0)
\;\xrightarrow{T1}\;
M_1=(0,1,1,0,0,0,0)
\;\xrightarrow{T2}\;
M_2=(0,0,1,1,0,0,0)
\;\xrightarrow{T5}\;
M_3=(0,0,1,0,0,0,1)
\]

So the sequence is:

**T1, T2, T5**

Why this works:
- After **T1**, tokens are in **p1** and **p2**.
- After **T2**, the token from **p1** moves to **p3**, so we have **p3=1** and still **p4=0**.
- **T5** needs:
  - a token in **p3**, and
  - **no** token in **p4** because of the inhibitor arc.
- Since **T3** is the only transition that can put a token into **p4**, and it has not fired yet, **p4** is still empty.

Therefore **T5 can indeed fire before T3**.
