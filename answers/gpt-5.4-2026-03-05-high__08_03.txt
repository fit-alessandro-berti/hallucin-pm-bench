Yes.

A **shortest** firing sequence is:

**T1, T2b, T4, T5**

One marking-by-marking witness:

- Initial:  
  \(M_0 = (p0=1)\)

- After **T1**:  
  \(M_1 = (pa=1,\; pb=1)\)

- After **T2b**:  
  \(M_2 = (pb=1,\; pd=1)\)

- After **T4**:  
  \(M_3 = (pd=1,\; pe=1)\)

At \(M_3\), **T5** is enabled because it needs exactly one token in **pd** and one in **pe**.  
Also, **T3** has not fired before this point.

So **T5 can fire before T3**.

Why this is shortest:
- **T5** requires tokens in **pd** and **pe**.
- To get **pe**, you must fire **T4**, which requires **pb**, so **T1** must have fired first.
- To get **pd** without using **T3**, you must fire **T2b**, which requires **pa**, again produced by **T1**.
- Thus before **T5**, you necessarily need:
  - **T1**
  - **T2b**
  - **T4**

So the minimum length is \(3 + 1 = 4\) transitions, and the above sequence achieves it.

(Equivalent shortest interleaving: **T1, T4, T2b, T5**.)
