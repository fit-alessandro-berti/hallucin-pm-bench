
Yes, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3. A concrete firing sequence from the initial marking is:

1. **T1**: \(p_0 \to p_1 + p_2\)  
   Marking: \(p_1=1, p_2=1\) (others 0).

2. **T2**: \(p_1 \to p_3\)  
   Marking: \(p_2=1, p_3=1\) (others 0).  
   Now \(p_4=0\), so T5 is enabled (inhibitor arc checks \(p_4=0\)).

3. **T5**: \(p_3\ -|\ p_4 \to p_6\)  
   Marking: \(p_2=1, p_6=1\) (others 0).

4. **T3**: \(p_2 \to p_4\)  
   Marking: \(p_4=1, p_6=1\) (others 0).

Thus T5 fires before T3 in this reachable sequence. The key is that after T2, \(p_3=1\) and \(p_4=0\), enabling T5 while T3 is still enabled but not yet fired.
