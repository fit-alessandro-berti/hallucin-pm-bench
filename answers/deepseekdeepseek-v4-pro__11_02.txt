**TO:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**FROM:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**DATE:** Q1 2025  
**SUBJECT:** Claims Handling – Three Worst-Performing Activities  

The Q1 dashboard highlights three activities requiring immediate attention based on wait time, rework, and SLA performance.

1. **Request Documents**  
   - *Symptoms:* Extreme average wait (150 min), highest rework rate (22%), and elevated SLA breach (5.5%). Queueing dominates despite a short processing time (5 min).  
   - *Root cause:* High rework loop. Repeated document requests inflate the queue and delay downstream steps.  
   - *Action:* Deploy a mandatory, system-enforced completeness checklist at submission to cut rework and reduce queueing.

2. **Review Documents**  
   - *Symptoms:* Longest processing time (25 min), highest variability (stdev 9 min), and worst SLA breach rate (12.4%). Wait time (30 min) is also elevated.  
   - *Root cause:* Unstandardized review creates unpredictable effort and frequent deadline misses.  
   - *Action:* Introduce a decision-support template with clear acceptance criteria and enforce work-in-progress limits to dampen variability and improve SLA adherence.

3. **Initial Assessment**  
   - *Symptoms:* Second-highest processing time (12 min) and variability (stdev 4), combined with a notable wait (18 min) and 3.2% SLA breach.  
   - *Root cause:* It acts as a bottleneck early in the process, and inconsistent handling increases queue buildup.  
   - *Action:* Rebalance capacity by cross-training staff from low-wait activities (e.g., Check Coverage) and standardize the assessment questionnaire to lower processing time deviation.

Addressing these three will yield the fastest improvement in end-to-end flow and compliance.
