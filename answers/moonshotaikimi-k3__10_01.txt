## RACI Matrix — FinCo Order-to-Cash Workflow

**Legend:** R = Responsible (does the work) · A = Accountable (owns the outcome) · C = Consulted (provides input) · I = Informed (kept up to date) · – = no involvement

| # | Activity | Account Manager (AM) | Compliance Officer (CO) | Finance Clerk (FC) | IT Support (IT) |
|---|----------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1 | Receive Application | **A/R** | I | – | – |
| 2 | KYC Review | C | **A/R** | – | – |
| 3 | Order Vetting | **A/R** | C | I | – |
| 4 | Payment Clearance | I | – | **A/R** | – |
| 5 | Record Update | I | – | C | **A/R** |
| 6 | Notify Customer | **A/R** | I | I | I |

### Rationale for key assignments

- **Single accountability per row:** Each activity has exactly one **A**, held by the role that performs the work — this avoids ownership gaps or duplicated sign-off.
- **KYC Review (Row 2):** The CO owns and executes this step; the AM is **Consulted** because they supply the customer documents and context gathered at intake.
- **Order Vetting (Row 3):** The AM owns data-completeness checks; the CO is **Consulted** on any compliance flags, and the FC is **Informed** so settlement can be teed up promptly.
- **Payment Clearance (Row 4):** The FC owns settlement initiation; the AM is **Informed** so they can time the customer confirmation correctly.
- **Record Update (Row 5):** IT Support owns the CRM refresh; the FC is **Consulted** to confirm settlement details before records are finalised.
- **Notify Customer (Row 6):** The AM closes the loop; all other roles are **Informed** so everyone has visibility that the order cycle is complete.

This matrix can be dropped straight into a process document or workshop pack. Let me know if you'd like an alternative split (e.g., separating A and R where a senior reviewer should hold accountability).
