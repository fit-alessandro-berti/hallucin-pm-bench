**Subject:** Q1‑2025 Claims Handling Performance – Actionable Insights  

**Key Findings (Worst‑Performing Activities):**  

1. **Request_Documents** – Avg. wait time 150 min (≈2.5 days), through‑put 190 cases/day, and a staggering rework rate of 22 %. The prolonged queue and high rework indicate bottlenecks in document capture and validation.  

2. **Review_Documents** – Avg. wait time 30 min, through‑put 185 cases/day, with a rework rate of 5.3 % and SLA breach at 12.4 %. The document‑review step experiences significant variability (σ = 9 min) and delays, leading to excess processing time and non‑compliance.  

3. **Initial_Assessment** – Avg. wait time 18 min, through‑put 270 cases/day, but the longest queue (18 min) among front‑line steps and the highest rework (1.8 %) and SLA breach (3.2 %). This stage is the primary source of early‑stage bottlenecks.  

**Recommended Actions (Data‑Driven):**  

- **Request_Documents:** Automate electronic document ingestion and implement real‑time validation rules to cut average wait time by ~70 % and reduce rework to <5 %.  
- **Review_Documents:** Deploy a rule‑based decision support tool to standardize criteria, targeting a 40 % reduction in processing‑time variability (σ) and lowering the SLA breach rate to <5 %.  
- **Initial_Assessment:** Introduce a triage workflow with AI‑assisted categorization, aiming to decrease the average queue by 50 % and rework rate to <1 %, thereby improving overall claim throughput.  

These targeted interventions align with the observed metrics and promise measurable improvements in both speed and quality.
