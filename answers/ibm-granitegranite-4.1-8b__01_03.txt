**a) Decision points (gates / conditional branches) in Acme’s SMALL‑BUSINESS loan‑approval process**

1. **Application receipt** – The process begins once Form 14B (the loan‑application document) is received.  
2. **Risk‑rating outcome (Tri‑Color‑Risk‑Check)** – After applying Scorecard‑Cyan, Scorecard‑Magenta, and Scorecard‑Yellow, the procedure assigns a risk rating of **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**.  
3. **Conditional skip of Manual‑Underwriting‑Review** – If the risk rating is **Low** or **Medium** **and** the requested loan amount is **≤ €250 000**, step 5 (Manual‑Underwriting‑Review) is bypassed; otherwise it proceeds.  
4. **TriVote Decision Gate** – The “TriVote” gate requires approvals from the **Credit Lead**, **Regulatory Liaison**, and **Sustainability Officer**. The loan passes if **any two** of these three approvals are obtained (i.e., the decision rule tolerates a “majority‑of‑two” outcome).  

These are the only points where the flow can diverge or be authorized.

---

**b) All required documents, listed in the order they first appear**

1. **Form 14B** – Application document received in step 1.  
2. **Deck Memo** – Produced after the Manual‑Underwriting‑Review (step 5).  
3. **Offer Sheet 77** – Prepared when the loan is ready to be presented (step 7).  
4. **ID Matrix Declaration** – Required when the applicant confirms the offer (step 8).  
5. **Loan Dossier 2025** – Final agreement document completed after applicant confirmation (step 9).  

No other documents (e.g., credit‑bureau reports or KYC/AML files) are part of the in‑house process as defined.
