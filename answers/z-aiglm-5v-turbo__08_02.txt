**Question 2 (bonus).**
**Minimal Firing Sequence:**
`[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]`

**Explanation:**
To fire **T5**, the net requires tokens at **P2** and **P5**.
1.  Fire **T1** (AND-split): Produces tokens at **P1** and **P2**.
2.  Fire **T2**: Moves token P1 $\rightarrow$ **P3**. (Token remains at P2).
3.  Fire **T7**: Moves token P3 $\rightarrow$ **P4**. (Token remains at P2; **T3** is enabled but skipped).
4.  Fire **T4**: Moves token P4 $\rightarrow$ **P5**. (Token remains at P2).
5.  Fire **T5**: Synchronizes **P2** and **P5** $\rightarrow$ P6.

In this sequence, **T5** fires successfully. Because **T5** consumes the token at **P2** (which **T3** requires), **T3** is rendered unable to fire afterwards. Thus, **T5** executes prior to **T3**.
