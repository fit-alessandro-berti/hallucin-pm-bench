Yes.

A **shortest** firing sequence is:

**T1, T2b, T4, T5**

Why this works:

- Initial marking: only **p0 = 1**
- **T1** fires: consumes `p0`, produces `pa` and `pb`
- **T2b** fires: consumes `pa`, produces `pd`
- **T4** fires: consumes `pb`, produces `pe`
- Now both `pd` and `pe` are marked, so **T5** is enabled
- **T5** fires, producing `pf`

Up to this point, **T3 has not fired at all**, so **T5 fires before T3**.

Formal reachability trace:

- \(M_0 = \{p0\}\)
- after **T1**: \(M_1 = \{pa, pb\}\)
- after **T2b**: \(M_2 = \{pb, pd\}\)
- after **T4**: \(M_3 = \{pd, pe\}\)
- after **T5**: \(M_4 = \{pf\}\)

So the answer is **yes**.
