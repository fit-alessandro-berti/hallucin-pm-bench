To enable **T5**, we need one token in **pd** and one token in **pe**.

From the initial marking \(p0=1\), the **shortest** way to achieve that *without firing T3* is to use the shortcut **T2b** (which produces \(pd\) directly from \(pa\)):

**Shortest firing sequence:**  
\[
\boxed{T1,\; T2b,\; T4,\; T5}
\]

**Marking evolution (brief):**
1. Start: \(p0=1\)
2. Fire **T1**: \(pa=1,\, pb=1\)
3. Fire **T2b**: \(pd=1,\, pb=1\)  (no \(pc\), so **T3** not enabled/fired)
4. Fire **T4**: \(pd=1,\, pe=1\)
5. Fire **T5**: consumes \(pd\) and \(pe\), produces \(pf=1\)

Thus **T5 fires before T3** (indeed, with this sequence **T3 never fires at all**).
