Short proof:

- Initially, the only token is in p0.
- To enable T5, two conditions must hold:
  1. p3 must contain a token, and
  2. p4 must contain zero tokens, because p4 is connected to T5 by an inhibitor arc.

- The only way to get a token into p3 is:
  - first fire T1, producing tokens in p1 and p2,
  - then fire T2, moving the token from p1 to p3.

- But after T1 fires, p2 already contains a token.
- Transition T3 is then enabled, and if it has not fired yet, that token remains in p2.
- There is no transition other than T3 that consumes the token from p2, so before T3 fires we necessarily have p4 = 0.

Thus after firing T1 and then T2, we reach marking:
- p3 = 1,
- p4 = 0,
so T5 is enabled even though T3 has not fired.

Concrete firing sequence from the initial marking:
- T1, then T2, then T5

Markings:
- Initial: (p0=1, others 0)
- After T1: p1=1, p2=1
- After T2: p3=1, p2=1, and p4=0
- Therefore T5 is enabled and may fire before T3.
