Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \;\rightarrow\; T2 \;\rightarrow\; T5
\]

Markings:

- Initial: \(p0=1\)
- After \(T1\): \(p1=1,\; p2=1\)
- After \(T2\): \(p2=1,\; p3=1\), while \(p4=0\)
- At this point, \(T5\) is enabled because it has a token in \(p3\) and its inhibitor condition requires \(p4=0\), which holds.

Thus \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\), producing a token in \(p6\).
