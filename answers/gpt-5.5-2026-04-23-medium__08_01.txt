Yes.

One concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1,\; T2,\; T5
\]

Step-by-step:

1. Initial marking:

\[
M_0 = \{p0\}
\]

2. Fire \(T1\):

\[
p0 \to p1 + p2
\]

So the marking becomes:

\[
M_1 = \{p1, p2\}
\]

3. Fire \(T2\):

\[
p1 \to p3
\]

So the marking becomes:

\[
M_2 = \{p2, p3\}
\]

4. At \(M_2\), transition \(T5\) is enabled because:
   - \(p3\) contains a token, satisfying the normal input arc.
   - \(p4\) contains zero tokens, satisfying the inhibitor condition.

Therefore \(T5\) can fire:

\[
p3 \;-\!|p4 \to p6
\]

giving:

\[
M_3 = \{p2, p6\}
\]

Thus, \(T5\) can indeed fire before \(T3\).
