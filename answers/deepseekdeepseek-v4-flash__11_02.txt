**MEMORANDUM**

**To:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**From:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**Re:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling – Key Bottlenecks

Three activities require immediate attention:

1. **Request_Documents** – Extremely high average wait (150 min), rework (22.0%), and SLA breach (5.5%). The queue time suggests insufficient capacity or poor prioritization; rework indicates frequent missing or incorrect document requests.  
   *Action:* Automate standard document checks and implement a prompted confirmation step before submission to reduce rework and wait.

2. **Review_Documents** – Highest SLA breach (12.4%) and second-highest wait (30 min), coupled with high rework (5.3%) and processing variability (std 9 min). The combination points to uneven workload and inconsistent review standards.  
   *Action:* Introduce triage rules (e.g., simple vs. complex claims) and dedicated review teams to reduce variability and shorten wait.

3. **Initial_Assessment** – Elevated wait (18 min) and moderate rework (1.8%) and SLA breach (3.2%). While less severe, the wait time is the third highest, indicating a bottleneck after registration.  
   *Action:* Add capacity during peak hours or shift low-complexity assessments to a streamlined path.

Focusing on these three will yield the greatest improvement in overall throughput and SLA compliance.
