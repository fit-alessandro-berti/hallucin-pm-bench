**Executive Memo: Q1 2025 Claims Handling Performance**
To: COO, Acme Insurance
From: Senior Process Performance Analyst
Subject: Top 3 Underperforming Claims Activities & Corrective Actions
The three worst-performing claims activities, with no close candidates for ranking, are:
1. **Request_Documents**: It has the longest average queue wait (150 mins, 5x the next highest activity wait time) and highest rework rate (22%, 4x the next highest activity), driving throughput loss and avoidable SLA breaches. *Action*: Add 2 dedicated adjusters to this team to cut queue wait to <20 mins, aligned with the next highest activity wait time, to resolve understaffing relative to inbound request volume.
2. **Review_Documents**: It has the highest SLA breach rate (12.4%), second-highest rework rate (5.3%), and highest processing time variability (9 mins stdev), leading to inconsistent customer outcomes. *Action*: Roll out a standardized 5-point document review checklist to reduce rework to <1% (aligned with adjacent steps' rework rates), cutting SLA breaches and processing variability.
3. **Initial_Assessment**: It has the third-highest queue wait (18 mins), third-highest rework rate (1.8%), and third-highest SLA breach rate (3.2%), slowing overall claims velocity. *Action*: Deploy a pre-assessment triage bot to auto-assign low-complexity claims to junior adjusters, cutting wait time to <12 mins (aligned with the step's own average processing time) to reduce queue backlog.
