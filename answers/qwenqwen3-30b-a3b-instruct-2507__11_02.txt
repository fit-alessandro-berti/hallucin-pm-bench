**To:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**From:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**Date:** April 5, 2025  
**Subject:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance Review  

**Top 3 Worst-Performing Activities:**  

1. **Request Documents**  
   *Why:* Lowest throughput (190/day), highest wait time (150 min), and 22.0% rework rate. The long queue delay and high rework indicate poor initial document requests or unclear customer guidance.  
   *Action:* Implement a standardized, digital pre-screening questionnaire to reduce ambiguous or incomplete submissions, targeting 15% reduction in rework.  

2. **Review Documents**  
   *Why:* High wait time (30 min) and 5.3% rework rate; though throughput (185/day) is moderate, processing time (25 min) is slow and variability (9 min) is high, suggesting inconsistent validation.  
   *Action:* Introduce automated document validation rules (e.g., missing fields, format errors) to cut review time and reduce rework by 40%.  

3. **Initial Assessment**  
   *Why:* Despite high throughput (270/day), it has the longest wait time (18 min), highest SLA breach (3.2%), and 1.8% rework—indicating early-stage bottlenecks and inconsistency.  
   *Action:* Roll out a decision-support tool with predefined rules to standardize assessments, aiming to reduce wait time by 10 minutes and SLA breaches by 50%.  

All three areas suffer from upstream inefficiencies: *Request Documents* creates backlog, which cascades into *Review* and *Initial Assessment* delays. Addressing these will improve end-to-end cycle time and SLA compliance.
