**Yes, it is possible.**

### Concrete firing sequence
1. **T1** fires: `{p0=1}` → `{p1=1, p2=1}`  
2. **T2** fires: `{p1=1, p2=1}` → `{p2=1, p3=1}`  
3. **T5** fires: `{p2=1, p3=1}` → `{p2=1, p6=1}`  

At this point **T3 has not yet fired**.

### Why this works
- After **T1**, tokens are in the parallel branches `p1` and `p2`.
- Firing **T2** moves the token from `p1` to `p3`. The other branch (`p2`) is untouched, so **T3** has not fired and `p4` is still empty.
- **T5**’s enabling condition is: a normal token on `p3` **and** zero tokens on `p4` (inhibitor arc). In the marking `{p2, p3}` this condition is satisfied.
- Therefore **T5** is enabled and can fire while **T3** remains unfired.

So the sequence **T1 → T2 → T5** is a valid execution where T5 fires before T3.
