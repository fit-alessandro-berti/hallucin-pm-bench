**MEMO**

**To:** COO, Acme Insurance
**From:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst
**Date:** Q1-2025
**Subject:** Claims Handling Process Performance Analysis

Q1 data identifies three critical bottlenecks in the claims process:

1. **Request_Documents:** This is the primary systemic failure point, exhibiting the highest wait time (150 min) and a severe rework rate (22.0%). 
   *   *Action:* Review the initial document request checklist to reduce errors and rework.

2. **Review_Documents:** This activity is the most unstable and slowest to execute, with the highest processing time (25 min), highest variability (stdev 9), and highest SLA breach rate (12.4%).
   *   *Action:* Implement standardized review templates to reduce processing variance and SLA breaches.

3. **Initial_Assessment:** While throughput is high, it shows significant queueing (18 min) and a higher SLA breach rate (3.2%) than subsequent steps.
   *   *Action:* Reallocate staffing to the assessment phase to reduce the 18-minute average wait time.

**Summary:** The process experiences a significant throughput drop-off starting at *Request_Documents* (falling from 270 to 190 cases/day), suggesting that document handling is the primary constraint on total cycle time.
