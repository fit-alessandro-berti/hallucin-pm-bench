To enforce the SLA within the existing process structure, we attach a **Boundary Timer Event** to the `Activity_CreateAccount` task to trigger an escalation if the execution exceeds the 48-hour threshold.

### Changed/Added XML Elements:

```xml
<bpmn:boundaryEvent id="Timer_SLA" name="48 Hour SLA" attachedToRef="Activity_CreateAccount">
  <bpmn:timerEventDefinition>
    <bpmn:timeDuration>PT48H</bpmn:timeDuration>
  </bpmn:timerEventDefinition>
</bpmn:boundaryEvent>

<bpmn:sequenceFlow id="Flow_SLA_Escalation" sourceRef="Timer_SLA" targetRef="Activity_AMReview"/>
```

**Justification:** By attaching a non-interrupting boundary timer to the service task, we leverage the existing `Activity_AMReview` to handle SLA breaches, ensuring management is alerted or involved when the "Create Account" step exceeds its allotted time.
