**Why the required metrics cannot be computed from the provided artefacts**

Artefact A supplies only the *header* of an event‑log (the list of column names) and explicitly states that **every row and every timestamp value have been removed for privacy**. Without the actual activity timestamps (`start_time` and `complete_time`), it is impossible to:

1. Identify which events correspond to the activity “Approve Invoice”.
2. Determine the duration of each “Approve Invoice” event (which requires the start and end times).
3. Calculate the average cycle time, its standard deviation, or any confidence interval.

Artefact B offers corporate SLA information (e.g., the historical average of 7.8 days from “Approve Invoice” to “Pay Invoice”), but this is an aggregate KPI, not a distribution of individual “Approve Invoice” instances. Consequently, Artefact B does **not** provide the granularity needed to compute the average, standard deviation, or confidence interval for “Approve Invoice” durations.

Because the essential time‑stamped data are absent, the requested quantitative analysis (average cycle time in hours, its standard deviation, and a 95 % confidence interval) **cannot be derived** from the given information. Any attempt to calculate these values would be speculative and therefore inappropriate. 

Hence, the only accurate response is to state the above limitation.
