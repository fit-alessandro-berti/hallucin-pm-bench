**Yes**, it is possible for transition T5 to fire before transition T3.

**Concrete firing sequence from the initial marking**  
\[
M_0 \xrightarrow{T1} M_1 \xrightarrow{T2} M_2 \xrightarrow{T5} M_3
\]

**Marking evolution**  

| Marking | p0 | p1 | p2 | p3 | p4 | p5 | p6 | Enabled transitions |
|---------|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|---------------------|
| \(M_0\) (initial) | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | T1 |
| \(M_1\) after T1 | 0 | 1 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | T2, T3 |
| \(M_2\) after T2 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | T3, **T5** (p3=1, p4=0) |
| \(M_3\) after T5 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | T3 |

**Justification**  
- T1 splits the initial token into p1 and p2.  
- Firing T2 moves the token from p1 to p3, leaving p2 untouched.  
- At \(M_2\), place p3 holds a token while p4 is still empty (T3 has **not** fired). The inhibitor arc of T5 (–|p4) is therefore satisfied, and the normal arc from p3 is satisfied. Hence T5 is enabled and can fire.  
- Throughout this sequence T3 never fires, so T5 indeed fires **before** T3.
