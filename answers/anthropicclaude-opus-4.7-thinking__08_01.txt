## Answer: **Yes — T5 can fire before T3.**

### Concrete firing sequence

| Step | Transition | Marking after firing (p0,p1,p2,p3,p4,p5,p6) |
|------|-----------|---------------------------------------------|
| 0    | —         | (1,0,0,0,0,0,0)  ← initial |
| 1    | **T1**    | (0,1,1,0,0,0,0) |
| 2    | **T2**    | (0,0,1,1,0,0,0) |
| 3    | **T5**    | (0,0,1,0,0,0,1) |

### Justification

- After **T1**, both p1 and p2 hold a token (parallel split).
- After **T2**, p3 holds a token while p4 is still empty (T3 hasn't fired yet).
- **T5** requires: a token in p3 ✔ and *zero* tokens in p4 ✔ (inhibitor arc satisfied).
- Hence T5 is enabled and fires — strictly **before** T3 has ever fired.

So the ordering T3 → T5 is *not* enforced by the net; in fact the inhibitor arc makes the opposite order (T5 before T3) not only possible but the only way T5 can ever fire, since once T3 fires p4 becomes marked and remains so until consumed by T4.
