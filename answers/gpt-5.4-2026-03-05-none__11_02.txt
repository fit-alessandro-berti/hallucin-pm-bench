**To: COO, Acme Insurance**  
**Subject: Q1-2025 Claims Handling performance hotspots**

The three weakest activities are:

1. **Request_Documents**  
   This is the clearest bottleneck. It has by far the **lowest throughput among upstream steps (190/day)**, an extreme **average wait time of 150 minutes**, and the **highest rework rate at 22.0%**. SLA breaches are also elevated at **5.5%**.  
   **Action:** Redesign the document request to improve first-time completeness—e.g., require a standardized checklist/template at request initiation. Cutting rework should directly reduce the 150-minute queue and improve throughput.

2. **Review_Documents**  
   This step shows the **highest processing time (25 min)**, the **highest variability (stdev 9 min)**, and the **highest SLA breach rate (12.4%)**, with throughput only **185/day**. This indicates slow and inconsistent execution.  
   **Action:** Standardize review using decision rules/checklists for common document scenarios to reduce variability and bring processing time and SLA breaches down.

3. **Initial_Assessment**  
   While throughput is relatively high (**270/day**), it has a notable **wait time of 18 minutes**, **processing time of 12 minutes**, and **SLA breaches of 3.2%**, making it the weakest of the early core steps.  
   **Action:** Add capacity or rebalance staffing at Initial_Assessment during peak periods, as its queue is materially higher than adjacent steps.
