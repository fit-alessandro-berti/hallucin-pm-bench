Yes, it is possible for **T5** to fire before **T3**.  
A shortest firing sequence (4 transitions) is:

**T1, T2b, T4, T5**

**Explanation**  
1. **T1** consumes the initial token in `p0` and produces tokens in `pa` and `pb`.  
2. **T2b** consumes the token in `pa` and produces a token in `pd` (the “shortcut” that bypasses `pc` and **T3**).  
3. **T4** consumes the token in `pb` and produces a token in `pe`.  
4. Now `pd` and `pe` each hold one token, enabling **T5**, which fires and produces a token in `pf`.  

At no point in this sequence is **T3** fired (or even enabled), so **T5** fires strictly before **T3**.
