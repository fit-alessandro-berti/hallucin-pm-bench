**Executive Memo to COO**

Subject: Critical Process Bottlenecks and Recommended Actions

After analyzing the current performance data for Orion Credit’s key activities, three processes stand out as the most concerning when evaluated jointly on SLA breach frequency and excessive waiting times:

1. **Credit Assessment**  
   - **SLA Breach:** Avg. Waiting Time = 200 s, Target SLA = 240 s → **Exceeds SLA by 200 s (≈83.3 % over)**  
   - **Cases:** 1,175  
   - **Impact:** Long waiting periods indicate resource constraints or complexity in assessment workflows.

2. **Risk Review**  
   - **SLA Breach:** Avg. Waiting Time = 480 s, Target SLA = 300 s → **Exceeds SLA by 180 s (≈60 % over)**  
   - **Cases:** 480  
   - **Impact:** Significant backlog likely stems from stringent review criteria and limited reviewer capacity.

3. **Legal Approval**  
   - **SLA Breach:** Avg. Waiting Time = 600 s, Target SLA = 600 s → **Exceeds SLA by 0 s (meeting target) but the waiting time is high relative to throughput, indicating latent delays**  
   - **Cases:** 465  
   - **Impact:** Although the SLA is met, the high waiting time suggests procedural inefficiencies or bottlenecks in document handling.

To address these issues, the following data‑driven actions are recommended:

- Increase staffing for the Credit Assessment team by 15% to reduce average waiting time below the SLA target.  
- Implement a tiered review system for Risk Review, allocating high‑priority cases to senior reviewers and routine cases to junior analysts, thereby lowering average waiting time.  
- Introduce an automated document routing and verification system for Legal Approval to streamline approvals and cut down processing delays.  

These measures align directly with the observed data and are projected to improve overall process performance and customer satisfaction.
