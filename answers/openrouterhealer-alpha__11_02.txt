**MEMORANDUM**  
**To:** Chief Operating Officer  
**From:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**Subject:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling – Critical Bottlenecks Identified  

Based on Q1-2025 dashboard analysis, the three activities with the most significant performance challenges are:

**1. Request_Documents**  
*Why it under-performs:* Extreme wait time (150 min) indicates a major bottleneck—likely due to delays in customer response or system prompts. The very high rework rate (22%) suggests poor clarity in initial requests, forcing repeated cycles.  
*Action:* Implement a standardized, dynamic document-request template with automated follow-up reminders and a pre-submission checklist to reduce rework and queueing delays.

**2. Review_Documents**  
*Why it under-performs:* High processing time (25 min) combined with significant variability (9 min) points to inconsistent reviewer expertise or document quality issues. The SLA breach rate (12.4%) is the highest, showing frequent overruns.  
*Action:* Introduce a tiered review system using AI-assisted document classification to triage straightforward cases, reserving senior reviewers for complex ones, thereby reducing average time and variability.

**3. Initial_Assessment**  
*Why it under-performs:* Elevated wait time (18 min) and the second-highest SLA breach rate (3.2%) suggest capacity constraints or complex case routing at this early stage. Processing time (12 min) is also relatively high.  
*Action:* Deploy a rules-based automated triage engine to pre-qualify claims and route them appropriately, reducing manual workload and queue times for assessors.

These actions are directly supported by the data: each targets the most extreme metric deviations while addressing the root causes implied by the performance profile.
