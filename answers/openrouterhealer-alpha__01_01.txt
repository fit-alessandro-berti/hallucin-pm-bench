### Decision Points in the Process
The process has a few key points where decisions or conditions influence the flow:

1. **Eligibility Validation Decision**: After the passenger submits a claim, the Triage Agent reviews it using the *FlightManifest* and *WeatherReport* to decide if the claim is eligible. This involves checking against historical flight data and weather conditions.

2. **Pre-Approval Decision**: The Finance Controller reviews the claim with an *EligibilityChecklist* to decide whether to sign off on the refund. This is a critical checkpoint before any payment can be considered.

3. **High-Value Branch Decision**: This is the most explicit conditional point. If the refund amount is **greater than €1,000**, the process branches to require a *Senior_Auditor_Review* with an *AuditTrail*. If not, the process skips this step and proceeds directly to payment after pre-approval.

4. **Payment Execution Decision**: The refund payment is only processed after *Finance_Controller_PreApprove* has succeeded, and for high-value claims, after *Senior_Auditor_Review* is completed. This involves a final decision to execute the payment once all approvals and documents are in place.

Note: The legacy "CustomerService_Agent_Decide" activity is not part of Contoso’s process, as per the description.

### Required Documents (Artefacts)
All documents are explicitly defined in the process:

- **RefundRequestForm**: Completed by the passenger when submitting the claim.
- **FlightManifest**: Used by the Triage Agent to validate eligibility.
- **WeatherReport**: Also used by the Triage Agent for validation.
- **EligibilityChecklist**: Used by the Finance Controller for pre-approval.
- **AuditTrail**: Required only for high-value claims (refund > €1,000) during the Senior Auditor Review.
- **BankTransferAuthorization**: Needed to execute the refund payment.
- **RefundConfirmationPDF**: Emailed to the passenger as part of the confirmation letter.
