Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1,\ T2,\ T5
\]

Markings:

- Initial: \(p0=1\)
- After \(T1\): \(p1=1,\ p2=1\)
- After \(T2\): \(p3=1,\ p2=1,\ p4=0\)
- \(T5\) is enabled because it requires a token in \(p3\) and zero tokens in \(p4\). It fires before \(T3\), producing a token in \(p6\).

Thus, \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\).
