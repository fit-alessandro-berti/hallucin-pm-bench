Yes.

One shortest firing sequence is:

```text
T1, T2b, T4, T5
```

Markings along the way:

1. Initial marking:

```text
p0=1
```

2. Fire `T1`:

```text
pa=1, pb=1
```

3. Fire `T2b`:

```text
pb=1, pd=1
```

4. Fire `T4`:

```text
pd=1, pe=1
```

5. Now `T5` is enabled because both `pd` and `pe` contain a token. Fire `T5`:

```text
pf=1
```

Transition `T3` has not fired in this sequence, so `T5` fires before `T3`.

This sequence is shortest because `T5` requires both `pd` and `pe`. Before any `T3` fires, `pd` can only be produced by `T2b`, and `pe` can only be produced by `T4`. Both require the initial split `T1` first. Therefore at least `T1`, `T2b`, and `T4` must occur before `T5`, giving a minimum length of 4 transitions including `T5`.
