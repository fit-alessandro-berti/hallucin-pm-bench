Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \; \rightarrow \; T2 \; \rightarrow \; T5
\]

Markings:

1. Initial marking:

\[
M_0 = \{p0\}
\]

2. Fire \(T1\):

\[
p0 \rightarrow p1 + p2
\]

So:

\[
M_1 = \{p1, p2\}
\]

3. Fire \(T2\):

\[
p1 \rightarrow p3
\]

So:

\[
M_2 = \{p2, p3\}
\]

At this point, \(T3\) has not fired yet, so \(p4\) is still empty. Transition \(T5\) requires:

- a token in \(p3\), which is present;
- zero tokens in \(p4\), which is true.

Therefore \(T5\) is enabled.

4. Fire \(T5\):

\[
p3 \; -|p4 \rightarrow p6
\]

So:

\[
M_3 = \{p2, p6\}
\]

Thus, \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\).
