Yes, it’s possible for **T5** to fire before **T3**.

### Why
After the initial split, place `pa` has two outgoing transitions:
- `T2 : pa → pc`  “normal” path  
- `T2b : pa → pd`  shortcut that goes straight to `pd`, skipping `pc`

`T5` only needs tokens in `pd` and `pe`. We can get `pd` via `T2b` without ever firing `T3`, and we get `pe` via `T4`. Once both are present, `T5` can fire.

### Shortest firing sequence
`T1, T2b, T4, T5`

**Marking trace:**
1. Initial: `{p0:1}`  
2. Fire `T1`: consumes `p0`, produces `pa, pb` → `{pa:1, pb:1}`  
3. Fire `T2b`: consumes `pa`, produces `pd` → `{pb:1, pd:1}`  
4. Fire `T4`: consumes `pb`, produces `pe` → `{pd:1, pe:1}`  
5. Fire `T5`: consumes `pd, pe`, produces `pf` → `{pf:1}`

At no point in this sequence does `T3` fire, yet `T5` fires successfully. 

So `T3` is not a prerequisite for `T5` because the shortcut `T2b` allows `pd` to be marked directly from `pa`.
