**MEMO — Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance**
**To:** COO, Acme Insurance
**From:** Process-Performance Analyst

The three worst-performing activities are **Request_Documents**, **Review_Documents**, and **Initial_Assessment**.

**1. Request_Documents** — The clear bottleneck. Average wait time is 150 min (far above any other step) and rework rate is 22.0%, the highest in the process. Throughput also drops sharply here (270→190 cases/day). *Action:* Implement automated document-request reminders and a structured intake checklist to cut rework and clear the queue.

**2. Review_Documents** — Highest SLA breach at 12.4%, longest processing time (25 min) and greatest variability (stdev 9 min), plus 5.3% rework. *Action:* Introduce a standardized review template/scoring rubric to reduce processing-time variability and breaches.

**3. Initial_Assessment** — The next-worst on SLA breach (3.2%) with notable wait (18 min) and rework (1.8%), though the gap to other steps is modest. *Action:* Add an early triage rule to route simple claims through a fast lane, reducing queueing.

Note: Beyond these three, remaining activities are tightly clustered on all metrics; data does not clearly differentiate a fourth underperformer, so none is named.
