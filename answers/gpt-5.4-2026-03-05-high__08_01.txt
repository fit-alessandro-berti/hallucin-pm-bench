Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1,\; T2,\; T5
\]

Starting from the initial marking

\[
M_0=(p0,p1,p2,p3,p4,p5,p6)=(1,0,0,0,0,0,0)
\]

we get:

1. **Fire \(T1\)**:
   \[
   M_1=(0,1,1,0,0,0,0)
   \]

2. **Fire \(T2\)**:
   \[
   M_2=(0,0,1,1,0,0,0)
   \]

At \(M_2\), transition **\(T5\)** is enabled because:
- \(p3=1\) so its normal input is present,
- \(p4=0\) so the inhibitor condition is satisfied.

Also, **\(T3\)** has not fired yet; its input token is still waiting in \(p2\).

3. **Fire \(T5\)**:
   \[
   M_3=(0,0,1,0,0,0,1)
   \]

So **\(T5\) can indeed fire before \(T3\)**. The key reason is that after \(T2\), place \(p3\) has a token while \(p4\) is still empty, which is exactly the enabling condition for \(T5\).
