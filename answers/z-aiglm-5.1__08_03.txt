**Yes**, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3.

**Explanation:**
Transition T3 requires a token in place `pc` to fire. However, place `pc` is only populated by transition T2 or T6. By choosing to fire transition `T2b` instead of `T2`, we can bypass `pc` entirely and place a token directly into `pd`. Because `pc` never receives a token, T3 is never enabled, allowing us to proceed to T5 without T3 ever firing.

**Shortest firing sequence:**
T1, T2b, T4, T5

**Step-by-step trace:**
1. **T1** fires: consumes `p0` (1), produces `pa` (1), `pb` (1).  
   *Marking: pa=1, pb=1*
2. **T2b** fires: consumes `pa` (1), produces `pd` (1). (Bypasses `pc` and T3)  
   *Marking: pb=1, pd=1*
3. **T4** fires: consumes `pb` (1), produces `pe` (1).  
   *Marking: pd=1, pe=1*
4. **T5** fires: consumes `pd` (1), `pe` (1), produces `pf` (1).  
   *Marking: pf=1*
