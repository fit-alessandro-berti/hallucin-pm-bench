**To:** Chief Operating Officer, Acme Insurance  
**From:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**Date:** April 10, 2025  
**Subject:** Q1 2025 Claims Handling – Priority Performance Actions

This memo identifies the three most significant bottlenecks in our claims process, based on Q1 data.

**1. Request_Documents**  
This activity is the primary constraint, with the lowest throughput (190 cases/day) and an extreme average wait time of 150 minutes. The data indicates a severe queue, likely caused by its exceptionally high rework rate (22.0%).  
*Recommendation:* Conduct a root-cause analysis on the 22% rework loop to eliminate the need for repeated requests, directly reducing queue backlog.

**2. Review_Documents**  
Characterized by high processing variability (stdev: 9 min) and the highest SLA breach rate (12.4%). This inconsistency directly impacts compliance and predictability.  
*Recommendation:* Implement a standardized review checklist and targeted training to reduce processing time variability and breach rate.

**3. Initial_Assessment**  
This stage exhibits notable wait times (18 min) and the third-highest SLA breach rate (3.2%). While not the worst on any single metric, its early position in the flow magnifies its downstream impact.  
*Recommendation:* Analyze assessment decision patterns to create clearer triage rules, reducing handling time and preventing delays for routine cases.
