**Yes.**  
Firing sequence: **T1, T2, T5**  

After T1 (parallel split) the marking is p1 + p2.  
T2 then produces p3 while p2 remains marked and p4 is still empty.  
At this marking T5 is enabled (p3 = 1 ∧ p4 = 0) and can fire immediately, before T3 has ever fired.  

(The subsequent firing of T3 is still possible from the resulting marking, but is not required for T5 to have already occurred first.)
