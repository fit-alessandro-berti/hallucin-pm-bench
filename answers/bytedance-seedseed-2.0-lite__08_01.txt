### Answer: Yes, it is possible.
#### Concrete firing sequence from the initial marking: `T1 → T2 → T5`
---
#### Justification:
1.  **Initial state**: Only p0 holds a token. The only enabled transition is T1. Firing T1 consumes the p0 token, and produces tokens in p1 and p2, leaving the marking `[0,1,1,0,0,0,0]` (p0-p6, 1=token present, 0=absent).
2.  After T1 fires, both T2 (requires p1) and T3 (requires p2) are enabled. We choose to fire T2 first: this consumes the p1 token, produces a token in p3, resulting in the marking `[0,0,1,1,0,0,0]`.
3.  In this state, p4 remains unmarked because T3 has not yet fired (T3 is the only transition that can place a token in p4). T5's preconditions are fully satisfied: the normal input arc from p3 holds (p3 has a token), and the inhibitor arc from p4 holds (p4 has 0 tokens). T5 fires before T3 is ever executed, which meets the requirement.
