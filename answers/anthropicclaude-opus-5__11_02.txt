**MEMO — Claims Handling, Q1 2025**
**To:** COO **From:** Process Performance Analytics

**Three worst performers**

1. **Request_Documents** — the process bottleneck. Average wait is 150 min (25× the process median) despite only 5 min of processing, and rework is 22.0% — by far the highest. Throughput also drops here, from 270 to 190 cases/day.
 *Action:* Automate document requests at intake with a mandatory checklist; target rework below 10%, which should remove most of the 150-min queue.

2. **Review_Documents** — worst quality/SLA profile: 12.4% SLA breaches, 25 min processing with a 9-min standard deviation (highest variability), plus 5.3% rework and 30 min wait.
 *Action:* Standardise the review with a scored template and route complex claims to a specialist lane; goal is to halve stdev and cut breaches below 6%.

3. **Initial_Assessment** — 18 min wait exceeds its own 12 min processing time, with 3.2% breaches (third highest).
 *Action:* Add capacity or shift-level smoothing at this step so wait falls under processing time.

**Caveat:** Remaining activities (Register_Claim, Check_Coverage, Approve_Claim, Calculate_Payout, Pay_Claim, Notify_Customer) are not clearly differentiated — all show low waits, rework <1%, and breaches ≤1.5%. The table does not explain the 270→190 throughput drop.
