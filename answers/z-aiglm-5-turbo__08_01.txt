**Yes**, it is possible. 

**Concrete firing sequence:** 
`T1, T2, T5`

**Justification:**
1. **T1** fires, consuming the token from `p0` and producing one token in both `p1` and `p2`. (Marking: `p1=1, p2=1`)
2. **T2** fires, consuming the token from `p1` and producing a token in `p3`. (Marking: `p2=1, p3=1`)
3. At this point, `T5` is enabled because it requires a token in `p3` (which is satisfied) and zero tokens in `p4` (which is satisfied because `T3` has not yet fired).
4. **T5** fires, consuming the token from `p3` and producing a token in `p6`. 

Since `T5` successfully fired in the third step without `T3` having fired at all, it definitively fires before `T3`.
