**Yes.** T5 can fire before T3 because T2 and T7 can produce a token in P4, which T4 moves to P5, while the original token in P2 remains available for T5.

Minimal firing sequence:

`[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]`

After T1: tokens in P1 and P2.  
T2 then T7 create a token in P4; T4 moves it to P5.  
At that point, P2 and P5 both contain tokens, enabling T5. T3 has not fired.
