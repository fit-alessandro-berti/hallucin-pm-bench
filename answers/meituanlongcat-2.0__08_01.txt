Yes, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3.

**Concrete firing sequence:** T1 → T2 → T5.

**Justification:**
- From the initial marking \( M_0 = (1,0,0,0,0,0,0) \), only T1 is enabled. After firing T1 we get \( M_1 = (0,1,1,0,0,0,0) \).
- In \( M_1 \), T2 and T3 are both enabled. Firing T2 yields \( M_2 = (0,0,1,1,0,0,0) \).
- In \( M_2 \), T5 has a normal arc from p3 (which has a token) and an inhibitor arc from p4 (which has zero tokens), so T5 is enabled. Firing T5 occurs before T3 is ever fired.  
  (T3 remains enabled but has not yet fired; the sequence can later fire T3, but T5 has already fired first.)
