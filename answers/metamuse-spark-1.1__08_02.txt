Yes. `T5` needs `P2+P5`. `P5` needs `P4`.

`P4` can be produced two ways:
* via `P2` by `T3` - consumes the `P2` needed by `T5`
* via `P1-P3` by `T2,T7` - leaves `P2` intact

So if `T3` fires first, `P2` is gone and `T5` is dead forever. `T5` can fire *only if* `T3` has not fired before.

Minimal witness with `T5` before `T3`:

**`T1, T2, T7, T4, T5`**

Reaching `M={P2,P5}` which enables `T5` while `T3` is enabled but not yet fired.
