Yes.

One shortest firing sequence is:

```text
T1, T2b, T4, T5
```

Marking evolution:

```text
Initial: p0=1

T1 fires:
  pa=1, pb=1

T2b fires:
  pb=1, pd=1

T4 fires:
  pd=1, pe=1

T5 fires:
  pf=1
```

So **T5 fires before any occurrence of T3**. In this sequence, **T3 never fires at all** before T5.

This is shortest because T5 needs both `pd` and `pe`:

* `pe` can only be produced by `T4`, and `T4` needs `pb`, which can only be produced by `T1`.
* `pd` can be produced either by `T3` or by `T2b`. To have T5 before T3, the only usable producer is `T2b`, which needs `pa`, also produced by `T1`.

Therefore, before T5 can fire without using T3, the net must fire at least:

```text
T1, T2b, T4
```

Then T5 can fire. Hence the minimum length is 4 transitions, and `T1, T2b, T4, T5` is a shortest valid sequence.
