**To: COO, Acme Insurance**  
**From: Senior Process-Performance Analyst**  
**Date: [Current Date]**  
**Subject: Top Underperforming Activities in Claims Handling**

Q1-2025 data reveals bottlenecks in the Claims Handling process, with throughput declining from 280 cases/day at registration to 179 by payout. The three worst-performing activities are Request_Documents, Review_Documents, and Initial_Assessment, based on elevated wait times, rework rates, processing times, variability, and SLA breaches.

1. **Request_Documents**: Severe underperformance stems from the highest average wait time (150 min) and rework rate (22.0%), contributing to a throughput drop to 190 cases/day and 5.5% SLA breaches. **Action**: Implement automated document requests to cut wait times by targeting the 150-min queue, potentially reducing rework below 10% as seen in low-rework activities like Register_Claim.

2. **Review_Documents**: High average processing time (25 min), stdev (9 min), rework (5.3%), and SLA breaches (12.4%) indicate inefficiency and variability, with throughput at 185 cases/day. **Action**: Standardize review checklists to lower stdev to under 5 min, mirroring Approve_Claim's low variability (2 min) and reducing breaches proportionally.

3. **Initial_Assessment**: Elevated wait time (18 min), processing time (12 min), and SLA breaches (3.2%) hinder early flow, despite 270 cases/day throughput. **Action**: Add parallel assessors to halve wait times to 9 min, aligning with Check_Coverage's efficient 4-min wait and curbing breaches.

These targeted fixes could boost overall throughput by 10-15%. Further analysis recommended.
