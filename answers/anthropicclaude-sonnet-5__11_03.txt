**MEMO: Process Performance Review – SLA Breaches & Waiting Time**

To: COO, Orion Credit
From: Senior Process-Performance Analyst

Three activities show the most severe combination of SLA breaches and high waiting time, warranting immediate attention.

**Legal Approval** exceeds its 600s SLA by 300s (avg. throughput: 900s), with the highest waiting time in the pipeline at 600s.

**Risk Review** exceeds its 300s SLA by 300s (avg. throughput: 600s), carrying a waiting time of 480s.

**Credit Assessment** exceeds its 240s SLA by 60s (avg. throughput: 300s), with a waiting time of 200s.

These three activities represent the largest gaps between actual and target performance, driven largely by waiting time rather than processing time itself.

Recommended actions:

- **Legal Approval**: Investigate the 600s waiting time as the primary driver of the 300s SLA breach; prioritize resource reallocation to reduce queue delays.
- **Risk Review**: Address the 480s waiting time, which accounts for the majority of the 600s throughput time, to close the 300s SLA gap.
- **Credit Assessment**: Reduce the 200s waiting time component to bring throughput closer to the 240s SLA target, narrowing the current 60s breach.
