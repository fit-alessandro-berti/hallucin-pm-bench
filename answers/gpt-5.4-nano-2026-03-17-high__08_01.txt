**Yes.** For example, the following firing sequence is possible from the initial marking and fires **T5 before T3**:

- **Initial marking** \(M_0\): \(p0=1,\; p1=p2=p3=p4=p5=p6=0\)
- Fire **T1**: \(p0 \rightarrow p1+p2\)  
  \(M_1\): \(p1=1,\; p2=1\) (others 0)
- Fire **T2**: \(p1 \rightarrow p3\)  
  \(M_2\): \(p2=1,\; p3=1,\; p4=0\)

At \(M_2\), **T5 is enabled** because:
- it has a normal arc from **\(p3\)**, and \(p3=1\);
- it has an **inhibitor arc from \(p4\)**, and \(p4=0\) (since **T3 has not fired yet**).

So we can fire:
- Fire **T5**: \(p3 \;-\!|p4 \rightarrow p6\)  
  This yields a new marking with \(T5\) fired while **\(T3\)** has still not occurred.

Thus, **T5 can fire before T3**.
