**Yes.** One firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \rightarrow T2 \rightarrow T5
\]

Markings:

1. Initially: \(p0=1\).
2. After \(T1\): \(p1=1,\ p2=1\).
3. After \(T2\): \(p2=1,\ p3=1,\ p4=0\).
4. At this point, \(T5\) is enabled because \(p3\) has a token and its inhibitor place \(p4\) is empty.

Thus \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\), consuming the token from \(p3\) and producing one in \(p6\).
