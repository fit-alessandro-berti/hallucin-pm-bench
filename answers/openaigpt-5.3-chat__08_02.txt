After **T1**, tokens are placed in **P1** and **P2**.  
Instead of using the **P2 → T3 → P4** path, the process can reach **P4** via the **P1 branch**.

Minimal firing sequence:
T1 → T2 → T7 → T4 → T5

Explanation (≤120 words):  
T1 produces tokens in P1 and P2. The P1 token can move through T2 to P3, then through T7 to P4, and through T4 to P5. Meanwhile, the token in P2 remains unused. When P5 is produced, both required inputs for T5 (P2 and P5) are present, so T5 can fire even though T3 has never occurred. Thus T5 can fire before T3 because P4—and consequently P5—can be generated through the alternative path P1 → T2 → T7 → T4 without consuming the token in P2.
