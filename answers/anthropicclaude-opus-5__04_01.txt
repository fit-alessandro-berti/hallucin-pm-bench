## Verdict: **Non-compliant**

### Justification

1. **Rule 1 — SATISFIED.** `PO_Amount` = €12 300 > €10 000, so dual approval is required. The trace shows `Approve_PO` by *Procurement_Officer* (#2, 10:15) followed by `Approve_PO_CFO` by *CFO* (#3, 12:02), both strictly before `Issue_PO` (#5, 12:07). Correct actors, correct order, correct position.

2. **Rule 2 — VIOLATED.** `Expedite_Flag` occurs at #4, which activates the exception branch. The waiver of `Quality_Check` is legitimately used (no `Quality_Check` in the trace — acceptable, since it becomes optional). However, the *compensating* obligation is not met: `Risk_Assessment` occurs at #9 (10:31), while the first `Ship_Goods` occurs at #8 (09:40). The risk assessment therefore happens **after**, not before, the first shipment. This is the substantive breach — goods left the site un-assessed while the quality gate was simultaneously waived.

3. **Rule 3 — VIOLATED.** `Goods_Received` occurs at #6. The next two activities are #7 `Move_to_Quality` and #8 `Ship_Goods`. `Three_way_match` only appears at #10, i.e. at distance 4 from `Goods_Received`, exceeding the permitted distance ≤ 2.

4. **Rule 4 — SATISFIED.** The only *CFO* event is #3 (`Approve_PO_CFO`); it is directly followed by #4 `Expedite_Flag` executed by *System*, not *Intern*. The single *Intern* event (#7 `Move_to_Quality`) directly follows #6 executed by *Warehouse_Clerk*. No CFO→Intern hand-off exists.

5. **Rule 5 — SATISFIED.** The final event is #11 `Archive_Record` (12:00) and no events follow it.

### Summary
Two independent breaches: **Rule 2** (Risk_Assessment sequenced after the first Ship_Goods on an expedited case) and **Rule 3** (Three_way_match at distance 4 instead of ≤ 2 after Goods_Received). Either alone is sufficient for a non-compliance finding; Rule 2 is the higher-severity item because the expedite flag simultaneously removed the `Quality_Check` control, leaving the shipment with no effective pre-dispatch safeguard.
