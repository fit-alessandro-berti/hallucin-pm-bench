**YES**, it is possible for transition **T5** to fire before transition **T3**.

The existence of transition **T2b** allows the process to generate a token in place **pd** directly from **pa**, bypassing place **pc** entirely. Since **T3** requires a token in **pc** to fire, taking the "shortcut" (**T2b**) means **T3** is never enabled, allowing **T5** to synchronize and fire once **pe** is also ready.

Here is one **shortest** firing sequence demonstrating this:

1.  **T1** (Splits flow: tokens move from `p0` to `pa` and `pb`)
2.  **T4** (Processes `pb`: token moves from `pb` to `pe`)
3.  **T2b** (Shortcut: token moves from `pa` directly to `pd`, skipping `pc`)
4.  **T5** (Join: consumes tokens from `pd` and `pe`)

**Sequence:** `T1, T4, T2b, T5` 
*(Note: Steps 2 and 4 (T4 and T2b) are independent and can be swapped)*
