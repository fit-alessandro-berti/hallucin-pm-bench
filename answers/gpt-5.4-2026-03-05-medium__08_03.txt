Yes.

One shortest firing sequence is:

**T1, T2b, T4, T5**

State evolution:

- Initial: \(M_0 = (p0=1)\)
- After **T1**: \(pa=1,\ pb=1\)
- After **T2b**: \(pd=1,\ pb=1\)
- After **T4**: \(pd=1,\ pe=1\)
- Now **T5** is enabled, so it can fire:
  - After **T5**: \(pf=1\)

In this sequence, **T5 fires and T3 has not fired at all**, so **T5 fires before T3**.

Why this is shortest:
- **T1** must fire first, since it is the only transition enabled initially.
- To enable **T5**, both its inputs are needed:
  - \(pd\), obtainable directly by **T2b**
  - \(pe\), obtainable by **T4**
- Thus, before **T5** can fire, the net must execute **T1**, **T2b**, and **T4**.
- So any sequence reaching a firing of **T5** has length at least 4, and the above sequence achieves that bound.

So the answer is **yes**.
