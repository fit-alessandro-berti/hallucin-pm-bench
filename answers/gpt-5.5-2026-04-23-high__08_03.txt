Yes.

One shortest firing sequence is:

\[
T1,\; T2b,\; T4,\; T5
\]

Markings along the way:

1. Initial:

\[
p0=1
\]

2. Fire \(T1\):

\[
p0 \to pa + pb
\]

Marking:

\[
pa=1,\; pb=1
\]

3. Fire \(T2b\):

\[
pa \to pd
\]

Marking:

\[
pb=1,\; pd=1
\]

4. Fire \(T4\):

\[
pb \to pe
\]

Marking:

\[
pd=1,\; pe=1
\]

5. Now \(T5\) is enabled because both \(pd\) and \(pe\) contain a token. Fire \(T5\):

\[
pd + pe \to pf
\]

So \(T5\) fires before any occurrence of \(T3\).

This sequence is shortest because before \(T5\) can fire, the net must produce both \(pd\) and \(pe\). Producing \(pd\) without firing \(T3\) requires \(T2b\), producing \(pe\) requires \(T4\), and both require the initial split \(T1\). Therefore at least three transitions must precede \(T5\), giving a minimum length of 4 transitions.
