**Yes**

One shortest firing sequence is:
**T1, T2b, T4, T5**

**Reasoning:**
1. **T1** fires: $p_0 \to \{p_a, p_b\}$
2. **T2b** fires: $p_a \to p_d$ (the "shortcut" path)
3. **T4** fires: $p_b \to p_e$
4. **T5** fires: $\{p_d, p_e\} \to p_f$

In this sequence, $T_5$ fires successfully because it only requires tokens in $p_d$ and $p_e$. Transition $T_3$ is never required to fire to satisfy the input requirements of $T_5$ if the $T_{2b}$ path is taken.
