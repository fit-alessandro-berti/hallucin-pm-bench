**To:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**Subject:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance Priorities

The three clearest under-performing activities are:

1. **Request Documents** — Highest queue time (150 minutes) and rework rate (22.0%), with throughput falling to 190 cases/day from 270 upstream. Its 5.5% SLA-breach rate is also elevated. **Action:** Pilot a standardized, first-time-complete document-request checklist and track whether rework falls below 10% and waiting time below 75 minutes.

2. **Review Documents** — Highest processing time (25 minutes), processing variability (9 minutes), and SLA-breach rate (12.4%). It also has a 30-minute wait and 5.3% rework. **Action:** Analyze SLA-breaching reviews by processing step, then standardize the highest-variance step; target a breach rate below 5.5%, the next-highest rate in the process.

3. **Initial Assessment** — Among the remaining activities, it has the highest wait (18 minutes), rework (1.8%), and SLA-breach rate (3.2%). Its throughput equals Check Coverage at 270/day, but its wait is 14 minutes longer. **Action:** Rebalance staffing or work allocation between these two 270-case/day activities, targeting Initial Assessment waiting time of four minutes or less.

These priorities are differentiated by multiple adverse measures rather than throughput alone.
