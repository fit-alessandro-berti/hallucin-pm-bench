**Executive memo — Q1 2025 Claims Handling performance**

The three weakest activities are **Request_Documents**, **Review_Documents**, and, less severely, **Initial_Assessment**.

**Request_Documents** is the clearest bottleneck: throughput drops from 270 to 190 cases/day, wait time is the highest at 150 minutes, and rework is extreme at 22.0%. Action: introduce a mandatory document checklist and automated completeness validation before submission, targeting a reduction in rework and queue buildup.

**Review_Documents** also under-performs: throughput is only 185 cases/day, average processing time is the highest at 25 minutes, variability is high at 9 minutes, and SLA breaches peak at 12.4%. Action: segment reviews by complexity and route simple cases to a fast-track queue with standard review rules.

**Initial_Assessment** is the third concern, though the gap versus other mid-process steps is smaller. It has an 18-minute wait, 12-minute processing time, 1.8% rework, and 3.2% SLA breaches—worse than Check_Coverage on each of those measures. Action: standardize assessment criteria and monitor assessor-level cycle-time variation to reduce delays and rework.
