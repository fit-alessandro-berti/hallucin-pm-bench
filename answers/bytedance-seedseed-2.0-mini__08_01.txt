### Answer: **Yes**, it is possible.

### Concrete Firing Sequence & Justification:
1.  **Initial marking**: Only `p0` has 1 token: `[1,0,0,0,0,0,0]`
2.  Fire `T1` (enabled, since `p0` has 1 token): This splits the token in `p0` to `p1` and `p2`. New marking: `[0,1,1,0,0,0,0]`
3.  Fire `T2` (enabled, since `p1` has 1 token): This moves the token from `p1` to `p3`. New marking: `[0,0,1,1,0,0]`
    At this point: `p3=1` (satisfies the normal arc requirement for `T5`) and `p4=0` (satisfies the inhibitor arc requirement for `T5`), so **`T5` is enabled *before* `T3` has fired**.
4.  Fire `T5` (enabled as above): This moves the token from `p3` to `p6`. New marking: `[0,0,1,0,0,1]`
5.  Finally fire `T3` (still enabled, since `p2` still holds its token from step 2): This moves the token from `p2` to `p4`.

In this sequence, `T5` fires immediately after `T2`, *before* `T3` has ever run.

---
Proof that this is valid: `T5` only requires 1 token in `p3` and 0 tokens in `p4`, which holds right after `T2` fires, before `T3` adds a token to `p4`.
