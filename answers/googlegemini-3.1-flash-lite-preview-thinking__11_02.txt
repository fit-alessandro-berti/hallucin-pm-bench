**MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**FROM:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**SUBJECT:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance Brief

Analysis confirms the following three activities are the primary drivers of process friction:

1. **Request Documents:** Suffering from a severe bottleneck, this activity exhibits an excessive **150-minute wait time** and a critical **22% rework rate**. 
   *   *Recommendation:* Audit intake requirements to reduce ambiguity and decrease rework-driven volume.

2. **Review Documents:** This activity has the highest **SLA breach rate (12.4%)** and significant variability (**9-minute stdev**). The long processing time suggests high complexity or manual review overhead.
   *   *Recommendation:* Implement an automated document-classification tool to standardize review criteria and reduce processing time variance.

3. **Initial Assessment:** While throughput is stable, wait times (18 minutes) paired with the highest frequency of upstream cases suggest resource constraints in early triage.
   *   *Recommendation:* Reallocate staffing resources from later stages to the assessment phase to alleviate queueing pressure.

Across the lifecycle, wait times in document-related steps account for the majority of our end-to-end latency. Addressing these document handling bottlenecks is essential for improving overall throughput and meeting our SLA commitments.
