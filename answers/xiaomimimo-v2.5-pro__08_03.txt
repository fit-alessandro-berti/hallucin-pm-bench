**Yes**, it is possible for transition **T5** to fire before transition **T3**. 

Here is one **shortest** firing sequence (length of 4 transitions) to reach a marking where T5 can fire, bypassing T3 entirely:

**Sequence:** `T1 -> T2b -> T4 -> T5` *(Note: T4 and T2b can be swapped, e.g., `T1 -> T4 -> T2b -> T5`)*

**Step-by-step marking changes:**
1. **Initial Marking:** `p0 = 1` (all others are 0).
2. **Fire T1:** Consumes `p0` and produces `pa` and `pb`. 
   *Current Marking:* `pa = 1, pb = 1`
3. **Fire T2b:** Consumes `pa` and produces `pd` (taking the shortcut and skipping `pc`/`T3`).
   *Current Marking:* `pb = 1, pd = 1`
4. **Fire T4:** Consumes `pb` and produces `pe`.
   *Current Marking:* `pd = 1, pe = 1`
5. **Fire T5:** Consumes both `pd` and `pe` (synchronising join) and produces `pf`.
   *Current Marking:* `pf = 1`

**Explanation:** 
By using the shortcut transition **T2b**, the process skips the `pc` place entirely. Since `T3` requires `pc` as an input, it cannot fire in this sequence. Thus, **T5** successfully fires at step 4 without **T3** having fired at all (meaning T5 strictly fires before T3). 

*(If it is required for T3 to eventually fire later in the sequence, you could subsequently fire T6 to feed back into `pc`, and then fire T3, but the 4-step sequence above is the absolute shortest path to enable T5).*
