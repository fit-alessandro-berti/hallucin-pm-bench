**Yes, it is possible.**

**Shortest firing sequence:**  
`T1, T2b, T4, T5, T6, T3`  
*(Note: `T2b` and `T4` are independent after `T1` and can be swapped without changing length.)*

---

### Step-by-Step Marking Verification
| Step | Transition Fires | Consumed Tokens | Produced Tokens | Resulting Marking (pd, pe, pc, pf, pa, pb, p0) |
|:----:|:----------------:|:---------------:|:---------------:|:---------------------------------------------:|
| 0    | `M₀` (initial)   | –               | –               | `p0=1`, others `0`                            |
| 1    | `T1`             | `p0`            | `pa, pb`        | `pa=1, pb=1`                                  |
| 2    | `T2b`            | `pa`            | `pd`            | `pb=1, pd=1`                                  |
| 3    | `T4`             | `pb`            | `pe`            | `pd=1, pe=1`                                  |
| 4    | `T5`             | `pd, pe`        | `pf`            | `pf=1`                                        |
| 5    | `T6`             | `pf`            | `pc`            | `pc=1`                                        |
| 6    | `T3`             | `pc`            | `pd`            | `pd=1`                                        |

At step 4, `T5` fires while `pc=0`, meaning `T3` is **disabled** and has not fired yet. `T3` only becomes enabled later via the feedback loop `T5 → T6 → pc`.

### Why This Ordering is Forced & Minimal
1. **Mutual Exclusion at `pa`**: `pa` receives exactly one token from `T1`. Since no transition produces tokens back into `pa`, `T2` and `T2b` are in direct conflict; exactly one can fire, never both.
2. **Enabling `T5`**: `T5` requires tokens in both `pd` and `pe`. `pe` comes from `pb` (`T4`), and `pd` must come from `pa` (`T2b`) *if* we want `T3` to fire later. If we instead chose `T2`, `pc` would be produced immediately, forcing `T3` to fire before `T5`.
3. **Feedback Necessity**: Because `T2` was skipped, `pc` starts at `0`. The only way to produce `pc` is via `T6`, which itself requires `pf` from `T5`. Thus, the dependency chain `T5 → T6 → pc → T3` is unavoidable in this branch.
4. **Minimality**: Any sequence where `T5` precedes `T3` must execute `T1`, `T2b`, `T4` to gather `pd` and `pe`; then `T5`; then `T6` to generate `pc`; then `T3`. This yields exactly 6 transitions, which is the theoretical lower bound given the dependency graph.
