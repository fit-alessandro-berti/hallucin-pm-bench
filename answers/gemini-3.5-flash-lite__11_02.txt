**MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**FROM:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**SUBJECT:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance Brief  

---

Based on the Q1-2025 dashboard, the three worst-performing activities in the Claims Handling process are:

1. **Request_Documents**  
   * **Underperformance:** Severe queueing bottleneck (`avg_wait_time_min` = 150) and massive quality failure (`rework_rate_pct` = 22.0%), leading to a high volume drop-off (throughput falls from 270 to 190).  
   * **Action:** Automate initial document checklist generation during *Initial_Assessment* to eliminate missing-information loops.

2. **Review_Documents**  
   * **Underperformance:** Highest SLA breach rate (`sla_breach_pct` = 12.4%), driven by long execution times (`avg_processing_time_min` = 25) and high variability (`stdev_processing_time_min` = 9).  
   * **Action:** Implement standardized document-verification templates to compress execution time and reduce variance.

3. **Initial_Assessment**  
   * **Underperformance:** Notable upstream queueing (`avg_wait_time_min` = 18) and the second-highest SLA breach rate (`sla_breach_pct` = 3.2%) relative to its front-end peers.  
   * **Action:** Reallocate staffing capacity from registration to assessment to absorb the incoming 280-claim daily volume without queue buildup.

*(Note: Remaining activities show stable flow, minimal wait times, and negligible SLA breaches, requiring no immediate intervention.)*
