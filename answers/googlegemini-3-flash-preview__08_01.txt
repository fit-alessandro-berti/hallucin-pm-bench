The answer is **Yes**. It is possible for transition T5 to fire before transition T3.

### Concrete Firing Sequence
1.  **Initial Marking ($M_0$):** `[p0=1, p1=0, p2=0, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0]`
2.  **Fire T1:** Tokens move from `p0` to `p1` and `p2`.
    *   *Current Marking:* `[p1=1, p2=1, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0]`
3.  **Fire T2:** The token moves from `p1` to `p3`.
    *   *Current Marking:* `[p1=0, p2=1, p3=1, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0]`
4.  **Fire T5:** T5 requires a token in `p3` and **zero** tokens in `p4`. Since T3 has not yet fired, `p4` is empty. The conditions are met.
    *   *Result:* T5 fires, consuming the token from `p3` and placing it in `p6`.

### Brief Justification
Transition **T3** is the only transition that can place a token in **p4**. Because **T5** is governed by an **inhibitor arc** connected to **p4**, the absence of a token in p4 is a strict requirement for T5 to be enabled. 

In a race condition between T2 and T3 (following the parallel split at T1), if T2 completes and produces a token in p3 while T3 is still pending (meaning p4 remains empty), transition T5 becomes enabled and can fire immediately. Once T5 fires, it consumes the token from p3, effectively "skipping" the synchronizing join (T4) that would have otherwise occurred.
