**No.**

T3 and T5 both consume the single token in P2 (produced once by T1). Firing T3 removes P2 and thereby disables T5 forever; firing T5 removes P2 and thereby disables T3 forever. Consequently no firing sequence can contain both transitions, so T5 cannot precede T3.

(The only sequences in which T5 ever fires are those that never fire T3 at all, e.g. T1, T2, T7, T4, T5; these do not constitute “T5 before T3”.)
