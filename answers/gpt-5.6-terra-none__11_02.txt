**To:** COO, Acme Insurance  
**Subject:** Q1 Claims Handling Performance Priorities  

The three clearest under-performing activities are:

1. **Request_Documents** — This is the primary flow bottleneck: throughput is only **190 cases/day** versus 270 upstream, and average wait time is the highest at **150 minutes**. It also has the highest rework rate (**22.0%**) and elevated SLA breaches (**5.5%**), despite only **5 minutes** of processing time.  
   **Action:** Redesign and automate document-request workflows (for example, standardized digital requests and completeness checks) to reduce repeat requests; target rework first because it is the largest measurable driver of delay.

2. **Review_Documents** — Throughput falls further to **185/day**, while processing time is the longest (**25 minutes**), variability is highest (**9 minutes**), and SLA breaches are highest (**12.4%**). Rework is also high (**5.3%**).  
   **Action:** Implement triage for document reviews, routing complex cases to specialist reviewers and standardized cases to a streamlined queue, to reduce processing-time variation and SLA breaches.

3. **Initial_Assessment** — It has the second-highest wait time (**18 minutes**) and relatively high SLA breaches (**3.2%**) versus most activities, with **12 minutes** average processing time.  
   **Action:** Add capacity or rebalance staff during peak intake periods, using the 18-minute queue as the operational trigger for reassignment.
