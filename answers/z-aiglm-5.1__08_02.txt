**Question 2.** T1, T2, T7, T4, T5

**Explanation:** 
T5 requires tokens in both P2 and P5. P5 is produced by T4 from P4, which can be reached via T2 and T7 from P1. Firing T1 provides tokens in P1 and P2. Instead of firing T3 (which consumes P2 to produce P4), we fire T2, T7, and T4 to move the token from P1 to P5. This leaves both P2 and P5 marked, enabling T5 to fire. Since T3 was never fired, T5 fires before T3 (and subsequently disables T3). Thus, `T1, T2, T7, T4, T5` is a minimal sequence demonstrating this.
