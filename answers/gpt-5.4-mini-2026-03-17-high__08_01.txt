Yes.

One concrete firing sequence from the initial marking is:

1. **T1**: \(p0 \to p1 + p2\)  
2. **T2**: \(p1 \to p3\)  
3. **T5**: \(p3 \rightarrow p6\) with inhibitor condition \(p4 = 0\)

Why this works:
- After **T1**, the marking is \(p1=1, p2=1\).
- After **T2**, the marking is \(p2=1, p3=1\), and still \(p4=0\).
- So **T5** is enabled because it needs:
  - a token in **p3**, and
  - **p4** to be empty.

Thus, **T5 can fire before T3**.
