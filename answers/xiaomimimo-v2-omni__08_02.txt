Yes, T5 can fire before T3. The token from P2 (produced by T1) must remain for T5, so P5 must be reached via the alternative path through P1 and P3. A minimal sequence is: T1, T2, T7, T4, T5. After T1, tokens are in P1 and P2. T2 and T7 move the token from P1 to P4 via P3. T4 moves it to P5, enabling T5 without firing T3.
