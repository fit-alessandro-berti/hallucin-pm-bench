**Q2. Minimal firing sequence:**

`[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]`

Explanation: after `T1`, tokens are in `P1` and `P2`. The `P2` token is kept for the later synchronising join `T5`. Meanwhile, the other branch fires `T2`, then `T7`, producing a token in `P4`; then `T4` moves it to `P5`. Now both required input places for `T5` contain tokens: `P2` and `P5`. Therefore `T5` is enabled and can fire before `T3` ever fires.
