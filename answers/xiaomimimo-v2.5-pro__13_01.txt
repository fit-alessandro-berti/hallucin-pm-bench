## 4. Two Sampling-Related Reasons the Monthly Estimate Is Unreliable

| # | Reason |
|---|--------|
| **1** | **Tiny, non-representative time window.** The fragment spans only a single day (~5 hours on 2025-05-01). A monthly estimate from one day's data ignores day-of-week effects, seasonal patterns, and long-term trends — the result would be highly sensitive to whichever day happens to be sampled. |
| **2** | **Very small sample size / low statistical power.** With only **4 cases and 18 events**, the observation of a single fraud check (1 occurrence out of 4 orders = 25% of cases) gives an extremely wide confidence interval. Drawing a generalization to an entire month's volume from a handful of cases is statistically unsound — a few more or fewer cases could swing the rate dramatically. |
