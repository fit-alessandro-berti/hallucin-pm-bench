**Yes.** T5 can fire before T3 because the token in P2 may remain there while the P1 branch reaches P5 via T2, T7, and T4.

Minimal sequence:

`[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]`

After T1, tokens are in P1 and P2. T2 → T7 → T4 moves the P1-branch token to P5, while the P2 token is unchanged. T5 then consumes P2 and P5 to produce P6. T3 has not fired (and cannot fire afterward because P2 is consumed).
