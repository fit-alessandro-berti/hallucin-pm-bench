**MEMO — Claims Handling Performance, Q1-2025**
**To:** COO, Acme Insurance | **From:** Process Performance Analytics

**Three worst-performing activities:**

**1. Request_Documents** — The process bottleneck. Average wait time is 150 min (vs. ≤30 min elsewhere), rework hits 22% (10× the next worst), and throughput drops from 270 to 190 cases/day at this step — losing ~80 cases/day.
*Action:* Replace manual document requests with an automated customer portal with upfront checklists, targeting the 22% rework rate that drives repeat requests and queuing.

**2. Review_Documents** — Highest SLA breach rate (12.4%), longest processing time (25 min), and highest variability (stdev 9 min), indicating inconsistent execution.
*Action:* Standardize review via structured checklists/triage rules to cut variability; monitor stdev and SLA breach monthly.

**3. Initial_Assessment** — Third-worst by SLA breaches (3.2%) and wait time (18 min), with elevated rework (1.8%). Note: it only marginally underperforms Check_Coverage and Calculate_Payout on some metrics, but its combined wait, rework, and breach figures make it the clearest third candidate.
*Action:* Add capacity or skill-based routing at assessment intake to reduce the 18-min queue.

Fixing Request_Documents alone could recover most of the lost daily throughput.
