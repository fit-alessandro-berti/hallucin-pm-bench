**Subject: Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance Review – Key Issues and Actions**

The three worst-performing activities in Q1-2025 are **Request Documents**, **Review Documents**, and **Initial Assessment**.

- **Request Documents** is the clearest bottleneck: it has the lowest throughput (190 cases/day), by far the highest average wait time (150 minutes), and the highest rework rate (22.0%). These metrics indicate frequent delays in triggering the activity and poor first-time quality.  
  **Action:** Implement automated document validation rules and pre-filled templates at the Initial Assessment stage to reduce rework by at least 50% within two quarters.

- **Review Documents** shows the second-worst performance with high processing time (25 min), high variability (stdev 9 min), and the highest SLA breach rate (12.4%).  
  **Action:** Roll out standardized review checklists and targeted training for high-variability performers to compress average processing time to ≤18 minutes and cut SLA breaches below 5%.

- **Initial Assessment** has the third-worst SLA breach rate (3.2%) and elevated wait time (18 min), indicating early-stage queuing pressure that propagates downstream.  
  **Action:** Increase capacity by 15% in this step (via temporary staffing or automation) to bring average wait time below 10 minutes.

These three activities are the primary drivers of overall process inefficiency. Immediate targeted interventions here will deliver the highest leverage for Q2 improvement.
