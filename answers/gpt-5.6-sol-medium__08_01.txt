**Yes.**

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \rightarrow T2 \rightarrow T5
\]

Markings:

1. Initially: \(\{p0\}\)
2. After \(T1\): \(\{p1,p2\}\)
3. After \(T2\): \(\{p2,p3\}\)

At this point, \(p3\) has a token and \(p4\) is empty, so \(T5\) is enabled by its normal input from \(p3\) and inhibitor condition on \(p4\). Thus \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\), producing marking \(\{p2,p6\}\).
