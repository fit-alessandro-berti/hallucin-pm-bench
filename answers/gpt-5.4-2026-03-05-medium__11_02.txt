**To: COO, Acme Insurance — Q1 2025 Claims Handling Performance**

The two clearest under-performers are:

1. **Request_Documents** — weakest queue performance: **150 min average wait** (far above all others), **22.0% rework** (highest by a wide margin), and throughput drops to **190/day** from **270/day** upstream.  
   **Action:** reduce avoidable repeats by introducing a required-document checklist at the request step; cutting rework from **22%** toward single digits should directly relieve the 150-minute queue.

2. **Review_Documents** — most execution-risk heavy: **25 min processing time** (highest), **9 min stdev** (highest variability), **12.4% SLA breach** (highest), and low throughput (**185/day**).  
   **Action:** standardize review with decision rules/templates and route complex cases separately; the goal is to reduce variability first, because high stdev and SLA breaches suggest inconsistent handling.

3. **Initial_Assessment** — the third slot is **less clearly differentiated**, but it is the next-worst based on **18 min wait** (excluding the document steps, the highest), **12 min processing time**, and **3.2% SLA breach**.  
   **Action:** add capacity or fast-track triage during peak periods to bring wait time down; its throughput (**270/day**) suggests this step is early and influential on downstream flow.
