Yes, it is possible for transition **T5** to fire before transition **T3**.  

In the following firing sequence, **T5** fires at step 4 while **T3** has not yet fired (and remains disabled because no token is in place `pc`):

**Shortest firing sequence:**  
`T1, T4, T2b, T5`

**Step‑by‑step marking evolution:**

1. **Initial marking** \( M_0 \):  
   \( p0=1 \), all others \( =0 \).

2. **Fire T1** (only enabled transition):  
   Consumes \( p0 \), produces \( pa, pb \).  
   \( M_1 \): \( pa=1, pb=1 \), others \( =0 \).

3. **Fire T4** (requires \( pb \)):  
   Consumes \( pb \), produces \( pe \).  
   \( M_2 \): \( pa=1, pe=1 \), others \( =0 \).

4. **Fire T2b** (requires \( pa \)):  
   Consumes \( pa \), produces \( pd \).  
   \( M_3 \): \( pd=1, pe=1 \), others \( =0 \).

5. **Fire T5** (requires \( pd, pe \)):  
   Consumes \( pd, pe \), produces \( pf \).  
   \( M_4 \): \( pf=1 \), others \( =0 \).

At this point, \( pc=0 \), so **T3** is not enabled. Thus **T5** has fired before **T3**.  

This sequence is the shortest (4 transitions) because **T5** requires both \( pd \) and \( pe \), which can be obtained only after firing **T1** followed by **T2b** (for \( pd \)) and **T4** (for \( pe \)). No shorter sequence enables **T5** without **T3** having fired.
