**Answer: Yes.**

**Concrete firing sequence**
\[
M_0 \xrightarrow{T1} M_1 \xrightarrow{T2} M_2 \xrightarrow{T5} M_3
\]

Markings (safe, 0/1):
- \(M_0\): p0=1
- \(M_1\) (after T1): p1=1, p2=1
- \(M_2\) (after T2): p3=1, p2=1,  p4=0
- \(M_3\) (after T5): p6=1, p2=1,  p3=0, p4=0

Here T3 has **not** fired yet.

**Brief justification**
1. T1 splits the token into p1 and p2.  
2. Firing **T2** consumes p1 and puts a token in p3; p2 remains marked, but p4 is still empty (T3 hasn’t run).  
3. At \(M_2\) the conditions for T5 are satisfied:
   - Normal arc: p3 contains a token.
   - Inhibitor arc: p4 contains **zero** tokens.
   
So T5 is enabled and can fire immediately, producing p6 and removing the token from p3, all before T3 ever consumes p2.

Thus T5 can indeed fire before T3. (Afterwards T3 may still fire from p2 to p4, but that is irrelevant to the ordering.)
