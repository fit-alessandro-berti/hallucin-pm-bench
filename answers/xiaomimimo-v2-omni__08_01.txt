**Yes, it is possible.**

**Firing sequence:**

1. **Initial:** (p0=1, others=0)  
2. **Fire T1** → (p1=1, p2=1)  
3. **Fire T2** → (p3=1, p2=1)  [p1 consumed, p3 produced]  
   At this marking: p3=1, p4=0, p2=1.  
4. **Fire T5** → consumes p3’s token, but inhibitor arc from p4 checks p4=0, so T5 is enabled and can fire.  
   Result: (p6=1, p2=1).

**Justification:**  
T5 requires a token in **p3** *and* **zero** tokens in **p4**.  
After T1 and T2 fire, p3 gets a token while p4 is still empty because **T3 has not fired yet** (p2 still holds its token, but T3 hasn’t consumed it).  
Thus, T5 can fire immediately after T2, before T3 fires.  

This sequence respects all arc rules:  
- T1 consumes p0, produces p1+p2.  
- T2 consumes p1, produces p3.  
- T5 consumes p3 (normal arc), checks p4=0 (inhibitor arc), and produces p6.
