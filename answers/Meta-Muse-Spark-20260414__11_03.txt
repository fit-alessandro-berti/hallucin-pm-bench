**Executive Memo – Process Performance Review**  
Date: April 14, 2026  
To: COO, Orion Credit  
From: Senior Process-Performance Analyst  

Analysis of throughput and waiting times against SLAs identifies three critical bottlenecks driven by both SLA breaches and excessive queuing:  

1. **Legal Approval** exceeds its 600s SLA by **300s** with throughput of 900s and the highest waiting time at **600s**, indicating severe capacity constraints.  
2. **Risk Review** exceeds its 300s SLA by **300s** with throughput of 600s and waiting time of **480s**, reflecting major delays relative to case volume of 480.  
3. **Credit Assessment** exceeds its 240s SLA by **60s** with throughput of 300s and waiting time of **200s**, creating downstream impact for 1,175 cases.  

**Recommended actions:**  
- **Legal Approval**: Reallocate two FTEs from lower-wait activities or introduce parallel review lanes to cut 600s average waiting time by at least 50%.  
- **Risk Review**: Implement automated pre-screening for low-risk segments, which represent ~60% of cases by volume drop from Credit Assessment (1,175) to Risk Review (480), to reduce queue entry.  
- **Credit Assessment**: Deploy workload balancing across shifts to address 200s waiting time, targeting reduction to sub-90s to meet 240s SLA for 1,175 cases.
