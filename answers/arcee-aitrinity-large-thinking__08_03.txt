
**Yes**, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3 in a reachable marking.

**Shortest firing sequence:**  
`T1 → T4 → T2b → T5`

**Explanation:**  
- Start from the initial marking `{p0=1}`.  
- Fire `T1` (uses `p0`, produces `pa` and `pb`). Marking: `{pa=1, pb=1}`.  
- Fire `T4` (uses `pb`, produces `pe`). Marking: `{pa=1, pe=1}`.  
- Fire `T2b` (uses `pa`, produces `pd`). Marking: `{pd=1, pe=1}`.  
- Now both input places of `T5` (`pd` and `pe`) are marked, so `T5` is enabled and can fire before `T3` ever occurs (note that `T3` has not been fired, and `pc` may never have been produced).  

No shorter sequence can achieve the required conjunction of `pd` and `pe` because at least one transition must create `pb` (`T1`), one must create `pe` (`T4`), and one must create `pd` (either `T2b` or the pair `T2`+`T3`). Adding the firing of `T5` itself yields a minimal length of 4.
