## Answer: **Yes**, T5 can fire before T3.

### Reasoning

T3 is only reachable via the path `pa → (T2) → pc → (T3) → pd`.
However, place **pd** can also be produced by the **shortcut transition T2b** (`pa → pd`), which bypasses `pc` and `T3` entirely.

Since T5 only requires tokens in **pd** and **pe**, and **pd** can be supplied via T2b instead of T3, T5 can fire without T3 ever having fired.

### Shortest firing sequence

```
T1, T2b, T4, T5
```

**Trace of markings:**

| Step | Transition | Firing condition | Resulting marking |
|------|------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| 0 | – | – | p0=1 |
| 1 | T1 | p0 → pa,pb | pa=1, pb=1 |
| 2 | T2b | pa → pd | pb=1, pd=1 |
| 3 | T4 | pb → pe | pd=1, pe=1 |
| 4 | T5 | pd,pe → pf | pf=1 |

At no point does T3 fire, yet T5 fires successfully at step 4. This is the shortest such sequence (length 4), since T5 requires both pd and pe, each requiring at least one prior transition (T2b and T4 respectively), which in turn require T1 first.

Hence, **T3 is not a necessary predecessor of T5** in this net — the shortcut T2b makes it possible for T5 to occur without T3 ever firing.
