**Q1:** Yes, T5 can fire before T3.

**Q2:** Minimal sequence: T1, T2, T7, T4, T5.  

**Explanation:** T1 produces P1, P2. T2 consumes P1 → P3; T7 consumes P3 → P4; T4 consumes P4 → P5. P2 from T1 remains. Now P2, P5 enable T5. T3 is not required to produce P4 because T7 already did. Thus T5 fires while P2 still holds, before T3 consumes P2.
