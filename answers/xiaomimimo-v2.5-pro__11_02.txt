**To:** Chief Operating Officer  
**From:** Senior Process-Performance Analyst  
**Subject:** Q1-2025 Claims Process – Performance Analysis & Recommendations

Based on the Q1 dashboard, the three worst-performing activities are **Request_Documents**, **Review_Documents**, and **Initial_Assessment**.

1.  **Request_Documents**: This is the process bottleneck. It suffers from a severe average wait time of **150 minutes** and a very high rework rate of **22.0%**, indicating frequent document gaps or errors that stall downstream work.
2.  **Review_Documents**: This activity has high variability (**stdev: 9 min**) and the process's highest SLA breach rate (**12.4%**), pointing to inconsistent handling or complex cases that often exceed service standards.
3.  **Initial_Assessment**: While less severe, it shows an early-stage delay with an average queue time of **18 minutes**, which contributes to overall throughput lag from the initial claim registration.

**Recommended Actions:**
1.  For **Request_Dobuments**: Implement a pre-emptive, digital document checklist portal for claimants to upload required files at submission, reducing wait and rework loops.
2.  For **Review_Documents**: Standardize review checklists and introduce a triage system to route simpler cases for streamlined processing, reducing variability and SLA breaches.
3.  For **Initial_Assessment**: Reallocate staff from lower-activity periods or cross-train agents to handle this queue during peak arrival times to reduce the initial wait.
