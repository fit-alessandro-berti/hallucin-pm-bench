## Yes — T5 can fire before T3

**Shortest firing sequence (4 transitions):**

```
T1 → T2b → T4 → T5
```

**Step-by-step token trace:**

| Step | Fired | Marking after firing |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| —    | (init)| p0 = 1 |
| 1    | T1    | pa = 1, pb = 1 |
| 2    | T2b   | pd = 1, pb = 1 |
| 3    | T4    | pd = 1, pe = 1 |
| 4    | **T5**| pf = 1 |

T5 fires while T3 has never fired — in fact T3 is not even enabled at any point in this run, since pc never receives a token (the shortcut **T2b** moves the token from pa directly to pd, bypassing pc entirely).

**Why this is the shortest possible:**
T5 requires tokens in both **pd** and **pe**.
- A token in **pe** can only come from **T4**, which needs **pb**;
- A token in **pd** can only come from **T2b** or **T3**; avoiding T3 forces **T2b**, which needs **pa**;
- Both **pa** and **pb** can only be produced by **T1**.

Hence at least three transitions (T1, T2b, T4) must precede T5, making any sequence shorter than 4 transitions impossible. (Steps 2 and 3 may be swapped: `T1, T4, T2b, T5` is an equally short alternative.)
