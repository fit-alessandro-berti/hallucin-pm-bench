**Executive memo — Q1 2025 Claims Handling performance**

The three worst-performing activities are:

1. **Request_Documents** — This is the clearest bottleneck: average wait time is **150 minutes**, far above all other activities, throughput drops to **190/day**, and rework is the highest at **22.0%**.  
   **Action:** Redesign the document request step with standardized request templates and automated completeness checks to reduce repeat requests and queue buildup.

2. **Review_Documents** — This has the highest processing time (**25 minutes**), highest variability (**9 minutes stdev**), and highest SLA breach rate (**12.4%**), indicating inconsistent and slow execution.  
   **Action:** Segment reviews by complexity and route simple cases to a fast-track queue with clear review checklists.

3. **Initial_Assessment** — While throughput remains relatively high at **270/day**, it has the next notable wait time (**18 minutes**), processing time (**12 minutes**), rework (**1.8%**), and SLA breach rate (**3.2%**) compared with most other non-document steps.  
   **Action:** Analyze assessment criteria causing delays and introduce decision-support rules to reduce handling time and rework.

The document-related steps are the primary performance concern based on the table.
