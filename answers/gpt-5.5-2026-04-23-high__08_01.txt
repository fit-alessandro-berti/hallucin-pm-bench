Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1,\; T2,\; T5
\]

Markings:

1. Initial marking:

\[
M_0 = (p0=1,p1=0,p2=0,p3=0,p4=0,p5=0,p6=0)
\]

2. Fire \(T1\):

\[
p0 \to p1+p2
\]

\[
M_1 = (0,1,1,0,0,0,0)
\]

3. Fire \(T2\):

\[
p1 \to p3
\]

\[
M_2 = (0,0,1,1,0,0,0)
\]

At \(M_2\), transition \(T5\) is enabled because:

- \(p3\) contains a token,
- \(p4\) contains zero tokens, satisfying the inhibitor condition.

Also, \(T3\) has not fired yet.

4. Fire \(T5\):

\[
p3 \; -|p4 \to p6
\]

\[
M_3 = (0,0,1,0,0,0,1)
\]

Therefore, \(T5\) can indeed fire before \(T3\).
