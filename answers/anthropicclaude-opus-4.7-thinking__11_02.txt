**MEMO – Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance**
**To:** COO, Acme Insurance
**From:** Process Performance Analytics
**Re:** Top 3 bottleneck activities & recommended actions

**1. Request_Documents — worst queueing and rework driver**
- Wait time of **150 min** (≈25× the process median) and a **22.0% rework rate** indicate customers are repeatedly asked for documentation, and requests sit idle before being issued.
- **Action:** Automate document requests immediately on claim registration via templated digital intake, with mandatory-field validation to cut the 22% rework loop.

**2. Review_Documents — worst SLA breach and highest variability**
- **12.4% SLA breaches**, the highest `stdev_processing_time_min` (**9 min** on a 25-min mean), 30-min wait, and **5.3% rework** signal inconsistent reviewer performance and insufficient capacity.
- **Action:** Introduce a standardized review checklist and rebalance reviewer workload; target stdev ≤ 4 min (in line with Initial_Assessment) to bring breaches under control.

**3. Initial_Assessment — second-largest queue, meaningful breach rate**
- **18-min wait**, **3.2% SLA breaches**, and **1.8% rework** — modest individually, but it is the first gating step feeding all downstream volume (throughput drops from 280 → 270 here).
- **Action:** Add triage capacity or auto-route low-complexity claims to fast-track assessment, targeting wait ≤ 5 min.

*Note: Differentiation between #2 and #3 is clear; the gap between #3 and Check_Coverage is narrower but Initial_Assessment leads on both wait and breach metrics.*
