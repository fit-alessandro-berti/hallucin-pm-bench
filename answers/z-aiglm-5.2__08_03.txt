Yes. A shortest firing sequence in which **T5** fires before **T3** is:

\[
T1,\; T2b,\; T4,\; T5
\]

Marking progression:

1. Initial: \(p0 = 1\)
2. After \(T1\): \(pa = 1,\; pb = 1\)
3. After \(T2b\): \(pd = 1,\; pb = 1\)
4. After \(T4\): \(pd = 1,\; pe = 1\)
5. After \(T5\): \(pf = 1\)

At this point, **T5 has fired** and **T3 has not fired**.

This is shortest because \(T5\) requires both \(pd\) and \(pe\). Initially both are empty; \(pe\) requires \(T4\) after \(T1\), and \(pd\) can be produced without \(T3\) by firing \(T2b\) after \(T1\). Thus the minimal necessary sequence is \(T1, T2b, T4, T5\).
