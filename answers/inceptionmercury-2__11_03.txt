**Subject:** Prioritizing remediation for the three activities with the greatest SLA breaches and waiting‑time exposure  

Our analysis of the current pipeline shows that three activities dominate both SLA overruns and waiting‑time delays:

* **Legal Approval** – average throughput 900 s vs. SLA 600 s (exceeds by **300 s**); average waiting time **600 s**.  
* **Risk Review** – average throughput 600 s vs. SLA 300 s (exceeds by **300 s**); average waiting time **480 s**.  
* **Credit Assessment** – average throughput 300 s vs. SLA 240 s (exceeds by **60 s**); average waiting time **200 s**.  

These three steps together account for the largest proportion of cases that miss their target SLA and experience the longest idle periods, creating a bottleneck that propagates downstream.

**Recommendations**  
- **Legal Approval:** Deploy a parallel‑review sub‑team to handle high‑volume cases, aiming to cut throughput by at least 25 % (≈225 s).  
- **Risk Review:** Implement an automated risk‑scoring engine to pre‑filter low‑risk applications, reducing waiting time by 30 % (≈144 s).  
- **Credit Assessment:** Introduce a decision‑tree workflow with predefined thresholds to streamline assessment, targeting a 20 % reduction in throughput (≈60 s).  

These data‑driven actions directly address the identified breaches and waiting‑time excesses, supporting faster case closure and SLA compliance.
