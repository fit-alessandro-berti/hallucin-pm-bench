A concrete firing sequence from the initial marking is:

\[
M_0 \xrightarrow{T1} M_1 \xrightarrow{T2} M_2 \xrightarrow{T5} M_3
\]

Using place order \((p0,p1,p2,p3,p4,p5,p6)\):

- \(M_0=(1,0,0,0,0,0,0)\)
- after \(T1\): \(M_1=(0,1,1,0,0,0,0)\)
- after \(T2\): \(M_2=(0,0,1,1,0,0,0)\)

At \(M_2\), **\(T5\) is enabled** because:
- \(p3=1\) (normal input available),
- \(p4=0\) (inhibitor condition satisfied).

So \(T5\) can fire **before any firing of \(T3\)**.

(If desired, \(T3\) can still fire afterward from \(p2\).)
