## RACI Matrix — High-Value Wire-Transfer Request

| Activity | Front-Office Analyst | Compliance Analyst | Compliance Officer | Treasury Ops |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Intake Vetting | **R / A** | I | — | — |
| KYC Review | C | **R** | **A** | — |
| AML Screening | I | **R** | **A** | — |
| Risk Scoring | C | **R** | **A** | I |
| Escalation Approval (score > 70) | I | C | **R / A** | I |
| Payment Scheduling | I | C | I | **R / A** |
| Funds Release & Analyst Notification | I | I | I | **R / A** |

**Legend:** R = Responsible (performs the work) · A = Accountable (owns the outcome, single per activity) · C = Consulted (provides input) · I = Informed (kept updated) · — = No involvement

**Notes on assignments**

- **Intake Vetting:** The Front-Office Analyst both performs and owns paperwork-completeness confirmation; the Compliance Analyst is informed so the file can be picked up downstream.
- **KYC Review** and **AML Screening:** Executed by the Compliance Analyst, with the Compliance Officer accountable as compliance-function owner. The Front-Office Analyst is consulted for KYC Review (source documentation and client context) and informed of AML Screening outcomes.
- **Risk Scoring:** The Compliance Analyst produces the 0–100 result; the Compliance Officer remains accountable. Treasury Ops is informed because the result determines whether the payment can proceed directly to scheduling.
- **Escalation Approval:** Applies only to cases scoring above 70; the Compliance Officer decides, with the Compliance Analyst consulted on the underlying KYC Review, AML Screening, and Risk Scoring evidence.
- **Payment Scheduling** and **Funds Release & Analyst Notification:** Owned end-to-end by Treasury Ops; the originating Front-Office Analyst is informed, and notification is an explicit output of the final activity.
