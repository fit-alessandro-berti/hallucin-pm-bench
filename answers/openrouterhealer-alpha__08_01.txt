Yes, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3.  
A concrete firing sequence is: **T1 → T2 → T5**.

**Justification:**

1. **Initial marking:** `m0 = (p0=1, p1=0, p2=0, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0)`.
2. **Fire T1:** consumes `p0`, produces `p1` and `p2`.  
   New marking: `m1 = (p0=0, p1=1, p2=1, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0)`.
3. **Fire T2:** consumes `p1`, produces `p3`.  
   New marking: `m2 = (p0=0, p1=0, p2=1, p3=1, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0)`.
4. At `m2`, transition **T5** is enabled because:
   - `p3` has a token (normal arc condition satisfied),
   - `p4` has zero tokens (inhibitor arc condition satisfied).
   - T3 is also enabled (due to `p2=1`), but it has not yet fired.
5. **Fire T5:** consumes `p3`, produces `p6`.  
   New marking: `m3 = (p0=0, p1=0, p2=1, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=1)`.  
   T5 has fired, while T3 remains unfired until this point.

Thus, T5 fires before T3 in this reachable marking.
