Yes.

One **shortest** firing sequence is:

**T1, T2b, T4, T5**

Using the place order \((p0, pa, pb, pc, pd, pe, pf)\):

- \(M_0 = (1,0,0,0,0,0,0)\)
- after **T1**: \(M_1 = (0,1,1,0,0,0,0)\)
- after **T2b**: \(M_2 = (0,0,1,0,1,0,0)\)
- after **T4**: \(M_3 = (0,0,0,0,1,1,0)\)
- after **T5**: \(M_4 = (0,0,0,0,0,0,1)\)

So **T5 fires, and T3 has not fired before it**.

Why this is shortest:
- **T5** needs one token in **pd** and one in **pe**.
- Before any **T3**, the only way to get **pd** is **T2b**.
- The only way to get **pe** is **T4**.
- Both **T2b** and **T4** require the tokens created by **T1**.

Therefore, any sequence with **T5 before T3** must include at least:
- **T1**
- **T2b**
- **T4**
- **T5**

So length 4 is minimal.  
(Also **T1, T4, T2b, T5** is equally short.)
