**Executive memo — Q1 2025 Claims Handling performance**

The three weakest activities are:

1. **Request_Documents** — This is the clearest bottleneck: throughput drops to **190 cases/day** versus **270** at Check_Coverage, with the highest wait time (**150 min**) and highest rework rate (**22.0%**).  
   **Action:** Redesign document request quality controls/templates and track missing/incorrect document reasons to cut rework.

2. **Review_Documents** — Performance remains constrained at **185 cases/day**, with the highest processing time (**25 min**), highest variability (**9 min stdev**), and highest SLA breach rate (**12.4%**).  
   **Action:** Segment reviews by complexity and create fast-track rules for standard/complete submissions to reduce average handling time and SLA breaches.

3. **Initial_Assessment** — The third-worst candidate is less sharply differentiated, but it has the highest wait time outside the document steps (**18 min**), relatively high processing time (**12 min**), and elevated SLA breaches (**3.2%**).  
   **Action:** Review staffing/queue allocation during Initial_Assessment and set a target to reduce wait time below 10 minutes.

Overall, the primary performance issue is concentrated in the document request/review loop.
