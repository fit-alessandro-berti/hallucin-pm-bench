**MEMO**
**To:** COO, Acme Insurance
**From:** Process Performance Analyst
**Re:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling — Top Three Performance Risks

Three activities materially underperform the rest of the chain. The data differentiates them clearly — they occupy the top three ranks on *both* rework rate and SLA breach.

**1. Request_Documents** — A 150-minute wait time and a 22.0% rework rate (≈4× the next highest) indicate that documents are being requested repeatedly or that cases stall before activity start. Throughput (190/day) sits one-third below front-end peers (270–280).
*Action:* Replace ad-hoc requests with a one-shot, automated packet plus a 24-hour escalation trigger for cases still queued at the 150-minute mark. Target: rework < 8%.

**2. Review_Documents** — Worst SLA breach (12.4%), longest processing time (25 min), and the highest variability (stdev 9 min). Output is both slow and unpredictable.
*Action:* Triage by complexity before assignment to flatten the stdev and defend a 12-minute processing target.

**3. Initial_Assessment** — Third-highest rework (1.8%) and SLA breach (3.2%), plus an 18-minute wait on the chain's first substantive step. Early-stage defects propagate downstream.
*Action:* Add a fast-track lane for low-risk claims and refresh intake-completeness training to push rework below 0.5%.
