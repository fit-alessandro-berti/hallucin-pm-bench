**Yes.**

One firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \rightarrow T2 \rightarrow T5 \rightarrow T3
\]

- After \(T1\): tokens are in \(p1\) and \(p2\).
- After \(T2\): tokens are in \(p3\) and \(p2\); \(p4\) is still empty.
- Therefore \(T5\) is enabled because \(p3\) has a token and its inhibitor place \(p4\) has zero tokens.
- \(T5\) fires before \(T3\), moving the \(p3\) token to \(p6\). \(T3\) may then fire using the token in \(p2\).
