**Executive Memo (199 words)**  

The three activities with the poorest performance are **Request Documents**, **Review Documents**, and **Check Coverage**.

1. **Request Documents** – Throughput is only 190 cases/day (the lowest) and average wait time is 150 min, indicating a severe bottleneck before any processing begins. The high rework rate (22 %) and SLA breach (5.5 %) further erode efficiency.  
   *Action:* Implement an automated document‑capture tool that validates completeness at intake, reducing the manual queue time and rework.

2. **Review Documents** – Although throughput matches Initial Assessment (185 /day), the average processing time (25 min) and standard deviation (9 min) are the highest, yielding a 5.3 % rework rate and 12.4 % SLA breach.  
   *Action:* Introduce a standardized checklist and targeted training for reviewers to cut average time by ~30 % and lower variability.

3. **Check Coverage** – Throughput is 270 /day, but the 4‑minute wait and 8‑minute processing time (relative to its role) keep the SLA breach at 1.5 % and rework at 0.6 %, making it the slowest of the high‑throughput steps.  
   *Action:* Deploy a rule‑based coverage‑validation engine to eliminate the wait and shrink processing time by 50 %.

These data‑driven interventions target the root causes identified in the metrics and should improve overall claims‑handling performance.
