# Executive Memo: Claims Handling Performance – Q1 2025

**To:** COO, Acme Insurance
**Re:** Bottleneck Activities in Claims Process

Three activities stand out as underperforming:

**1. Request_Documents** — The dominant bottleneck: wait time of 150 min dwarfs all others, and rework rate (22.0%) is the highest in the process, indicating repeated document requests delay cases upstream. *Action:* Implement an automated document-checklist/portal at intake to reduce round-trips and cut wait time.

**2. Review_Documents** — Highest SLA breach rate (12.4%) combined with the longest processing time (25 min) and highest variability (stdev 9 min), signaling inconsistent case complexity or reviewer skill gaps. *Action:* Introduce tiered triage (simple vs. complex claims) and standardized review checklists to reduce variance and breaches.

**3. Initial_Assessment** — Not a standout on any single metric, but its 18-min wait and 1.8% rework combined suggest moderate queuing inefficiency. *Note: the numbers here are less differentiating than for the top two candidates* — this ranking is tentative pending further diagnostic review (e.g., staffing levels at this stage).

**Priority:** Address Request_Documents and Review_Documents first; they show the clearest, most severe deviations in the data.
