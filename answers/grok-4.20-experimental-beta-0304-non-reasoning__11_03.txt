**Memo to the COO**

Our analysis of the credit process reveals that Risk Review, Legal Approval, and Credit Assessment are the three worst-performing activities when jointly considering SLA breaches and high waiting times. 

Risk Review exceeds its 300 s SLA by 300 s on average (600 s throughput) while incurring 480 s of waiting time. Legal Approval exceeds its 600 s SLA by 300 s (900 s throughput) with 600 s waiting time. Credit Assessment exceeds its 240 s SLA by 60 s (300 s throughput) with 200 s waiting time. These three activities alone account for the largest combined SLA violations and waiting-time accumulations across the 1,200-case dataset.

- For Risk Review, reallocate resources to reduce average waiting time from 480 s toward the current 120 s gap to SLA.
- For Legal Approval, implement parallel processing of low-risk files to cut the 600 s waiting time that drives the 300 s SLA overrun.
- For Credit Assessment, introduce automated rule-based pre-screening to lower the 200 s waiting time and bring throughput within the 240 s target.
