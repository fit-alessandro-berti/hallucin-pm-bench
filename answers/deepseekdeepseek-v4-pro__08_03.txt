Yes, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3.  
A shortest firing sequence is: **T1, T2b, T4, T5**.

**Explanation:**  
After firing T1, we have tokens in *pa* and *pb*.  
- Firing **T2b** consumes the token from *pa* and produces a token in *pd* (this bypasses T2 and T3 entirely).  
- Firing **T4** consumes the token from *pb* and produces a token in *pe*.  
Now both *pd* and *pe* are marked, enabling **T5**, which can fire without T3 ever having been enabled or fired.
