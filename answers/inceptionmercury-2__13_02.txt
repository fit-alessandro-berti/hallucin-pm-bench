**3. Why low‑support activities can mislead analyses (≤ 100 words)**  
When an activity appears in only a single case, its statistical weight is negligible. Models trained on such a slice may treat the activity as noise or overfit to it, distorting performance metrics (e.g., precision, recall) and causing unreliable predictions for future cases where the activity might be more common. Moreover, any derived process metrics (e.g., average duration, transition probabilities) become biased, potentially masking true bottlenecks or risk factors. Generalising from this limited evidence can therefore lead to erroneous business decisions.
