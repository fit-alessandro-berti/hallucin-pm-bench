Yes.  The shortcut `T2b` makes it possible.

`T5` needs `pd+pe`.

* `pe` needs `T1` + `T4`
* `pd` can be produced in two ways:
  * `T1 + T2 + T3` - normal path - needs `T3`
  * `T1 + T2b` - shortcut - does **not** need `T3`

Hence if we take the shortcut, `pd` can be obtained without firing `T3`.

#### Shortest witness for "T5 fires while T3 has never fired"

Marking as multiset.

* `M0 = {p0}`
* `M0 --T1--> M1 = {pa,pb}`
* `M1 --T2b--> M2 = {pb,pd}`  `pa` consumed, `pd` produced. `T2,T3` not enabled.
* `M2 --T4--> M3 = {pd,pe}` `pb` consumed
* `M3 --T5--> M4 = {pf}` 

Now enabled: `T6`. `T3` is *not* enabled, never was.

`T4` and `T2b` are concurrent, so `T1,T4,T2b,T5` is also shortest.

Length 4 is minimal: `T5` needs `pd` and `pe`, so it needs at least `T1` to get `pa,pb`, one transition to get `pd` and one to get `pe`. 3 transitions before `T5`.

This already shows a trace where an occurrence of `T5` precedes *any* occurrence of `T3`.

If you require a trace containing **both** `T5` and `T3` with `T5` first:

> `sigma = T1 ; T2b ; T4 ; T5 ; T6 ; T3`

* after `T5` : `{pf}`
* after `T6` : `{pc}` which enables `T3`
* after `T3` : `{pd}`

So `T5` at pos 4, `T3` at pos 6 first occurrence.

Remark: Immediate precedence `...T5,T3...` is impossible because `T5` produces `pf` not `pc`, `T3` needs `pc`. You need `T6` in between.
