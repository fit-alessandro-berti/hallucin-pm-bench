**Yes.** T5 can fire before T3.

Reason: after **T1**, the token in **P2** can be kept unchanged while the **P1** branch produces **P5**:
- **T1**: \(P0 \to P1,P2\)
- **T2**: \(P1 \to P3\)
- **T7**: \(P3 \to P4\)
- **T4**: \(P4 \to P5\)

Now the marking contains **P2** and **P5**, which are exactly the preset of **T5**, so **T5** is enabled even though **T3** has not fired.

A minimal witness sequence is:

**[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]**
