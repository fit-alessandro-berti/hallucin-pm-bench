**Question 2.**  
Minimal firing sequence: `[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]`

**Explanation:**  
1. **T1** fires, producing one token in **P1** and one in **P2**.
2. **T2** consumes P1 to place a token in **P3**.
3. **T7** consumes P3 to place a token in **P4** (bypassing T3 entirely).
4. **T4** consumes P4 to place a token in **P5**.
5. Both **P2** and **P5** now hold a token, enabling **T5** to fire before (and without) T3. 

*(Note: Firing T3 would actually consume the only token in P2, permanently preventing T5 from firing.)*
