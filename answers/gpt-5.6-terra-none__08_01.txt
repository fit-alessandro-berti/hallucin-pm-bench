Yes.

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \;\rightarrow\; T2 \;\rightarrow\; T5
\]

Markings:

1. Initial marking:
   \[
   M_0 = \{p0\}
   \]

2. Fire \(T1\):
   \[
   M_1 = \{p1, p2\}
   \]

3. Fire \(T2\):
   \[
   M_2 = \{p2, p3\}
   \]

4. At \(M_2\), \(T5\) is enabled because:
   - \(p3\) contains a token, satisfying its normal input arc; and
   - \(p4\) is empty, satisfying the inhibitor arc condition.

Therefore, \(T5\) may fire before \(T3\), yielding:

\[
M_3 = \{p2, p6\}.
\]

The token in \(p2\) remains available for \(T3\) afterward, but \(T5\) has already fired first.
