# RACI Matrix — FinCo Order-to-Cash Workflow

| # | Activity | AM (Account Manager) | CO (Compliance Officer) | FC (Finance Clerk) | IT (IT Support) |
|---|----------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1 | Receive Application | **R / A** | I | – | – |
| 2 | KYC Review | C | **R / A** | – | – |
| 3 | Order Vetting | **R / A** | C | I | – |
| 4 | Payment Clearance | I | – | **R / A** | – |
| 5 | Record Update | I | – | C | **R / A** |
| 6 | Notify Customer | **R / A** | – | I | I |

## Legend
- **R** – Responsible (performs the work)
- **A** – Accountable (owns the outcome; one per activity)
- **C** – Consulted (provides input before/during the task)
- **I** – Informed (kept up to date on progress/completion)

## Notes on key assignments
1. **Receive Application** — The AM owns intake; the CO is informed so KYC Review can be queued once documents arrive.
2. **KYC Review** — The CO is both Responsible and Accountable; the AM is consulted for customer context.
3. **Order Vetting** — The AM checks data completeness, consulting the CO on any compliance flags; the FC is informed that settlement is upcoming.
4. **Payment Clearance** — The FC initiates settlement and owns the outcome; the AM is informed for customer-relationship continuity.
5. **Record Update** — IT refreshes the CRM, consulting the FC to confirm settlement details; the AM is informed before notification.
6. **Notify Customer** — The AM emails confirmation; the FC and IT are informed to close the loop.

Each activity has exactly one Accountable role, ensuring clear ownership across the workflow.
