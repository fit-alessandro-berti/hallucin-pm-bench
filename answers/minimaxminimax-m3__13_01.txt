## 4. Two sampling-related reasons the monthly estimate is unreliable

1. **Extremely small sample size and coverage.** The fragment contains only 18 events from 4 cases observed over a single day. Estimating a *monthly* frequency from 1 occurrence observed in roughly 5 hours of activity provides almost no statistical power — confidence intervals would be enormous, and the single observed event could easily be a one-off rather than a representative rate.

2. **Non-representative, single-day time window.** The data covers only 2025-05-01, so it cannot capture day-of-week effects, weekly/monthly seasonality, or variation in fraud-check triggers across the month. A one-day snapshot is also vulnerable to selection bias (we have no evidence this day is typical), making any extrapolation to a full month unreliable.

(Additional valid concern: the sample is not a random draw from a defined population — it is an arbitrary log fragment, so its sampling design is unknown.)
