Yes.

A shortest firing sequence in which **T5 fires before any firing of T3** is:

```text
T1, T2b, T4, T5
```

Marking evolution:

1. Initial: `p0 = 1`
2. Fire `T1`: produces tokens in `pa` and `pb`
3. Fire `T2b`: consumes `pa`, produces `pd`
4. Fire `T4`: consumes `pb`, produces `pe`
5. Now `pd = 1` and `pe = 1`, so `T5` is enabled
6. Fire `T5`

Thus `T5` can fire without `T3` having fired at all. The shortcut transition `T2b` supplies the required token in `pd`, bypassing `pc` and therefore bypassing `T3`.

So the answer is **yes**, with shortest sequence:

```text
T1, T2b, T4, T5
```
