Orion’s process data indicates three activities where performance is weakest when considering both SLA breach and high waiting time: Legal Approval, Risk Review, and Credit Assessment.

Legal Approval shows the most severe delay profile. Its average throughput time is 900 seconds against a 600‑second SLA, exceeding the SLA by 300 seconds, while its average waiting time is 600 seconds across 465 cases. Risk Review also exceeds its SLA by 300 seconds (600 seconds throughput vs. 300‑second SLA) and has a 480‑second average waiting time across 480 cases. Credit Assessment exceeds its SLA by 60 seconds (300 seconds throughput vs. 240‑second SLA) and accumulates 200 seconds of average waiting time across 1,175 cases. In each case, waiting time represents a large share of total throughput time, indicating queue buildup rather than only execution time as the main driver of delay.

Recommendations:
- Legal Approval: Reduce the 600‑second waiting time by prioritizing its queue, as this waiting component alone equals the entire 600‑second SLA.
- Risk Review: Rebalance workload from earlier steps feeding its 480 cases to limit the 480‑second queue forming before the activity.
- Credit Assessment: Introduce queue management for the 1,175 cases to cut the 200‑second waiting time that drives its 60‑second SLA breach.
