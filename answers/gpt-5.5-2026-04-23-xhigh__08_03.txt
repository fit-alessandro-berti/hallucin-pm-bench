Yes.

One shortest firing sequence is:

```text
T1, T2b, T4, T5
```

Marking evolution:

1. Initial marking: `p0 = 1`
2. Fire `T1`: consumes `p0`, produces `pa` and `pb`
   ```text
   pa = 1, pb = 1
   ```
3. Fire `T2b`: consumes `pa`, produces `pd`
   ```text
   pb = 1, pd = 1
   ```
4. Fire `T4`: consumes `pb`, produces `pe`
   ```text
   pd = 1, pe = 1
   ```
5. Fire `T5`: consumes `pd` and `pe`, produces `pf`

So `T5` fires without any prior firing of `T3`.

This is shortest because `T5` requires both `pd` and `pe`; obtaining them before `T3` requires:

- `T1` to create `pa` and `pb`
- `T2b` to create `pd` from `pa`
- `T4` to create `pe` from `pb`
- then `T5`

Therefore, the shortest sequence has length 4.
