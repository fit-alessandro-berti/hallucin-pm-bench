**Yes.**

A concrete firing sequence is:

\[
T1 \rightarrow T2 \rightarrow T5 \rightarrow T3
\]

Markings:

1. Initially: \(\{p0\}\)
2. After \(T1\): \(\{p1,p2\}\)
3. After \(T2\): \(\{p2,p3\}\)
4. At this point, \(T5\) is enabled because \(p3\) has a token and \(p4\) is empty. Thus \(T5\) can fire before \(T3\).
5. After \(T5\): \(\{p2,p6\}\), and \(T3\) may subsequently fire.
