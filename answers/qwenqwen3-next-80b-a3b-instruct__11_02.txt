**Memo: Q1-2025 Claims Handling Performance Highlights**  
To: COO, Acme Insurance  
From: Senior Process-Performance Analyst  

The three worst-performing activities are:  

1. **Request_Documents** (avg_throughput: 190, avg_wait_time: 150 min, rework_rate: 22.0%) – Severe bottlenecks arise from prolonged customer delays and high rework, indicating unclear documentation requirements or inefficient follow-up.  
→ *Action: Implement automated, context-aware document checklists with AI-driven validation to reduce rework by 50% within 60 days.*  

2. **Review_Documents** (avg_processing_time: 25 min, stdev: 9 min, sla_breach: 12.4%) – High variability and SLA breaches suggest inconsistent reviewer capability or ambiguous evaluation criteria.  
→ *Action: Introduce tiered reviewer certification with real-time quality scoring to standardize processing and reduce SLA breaches by 40%.*  

3. **Initial_Assessment** (sla_breach: 3.2%, avg_wait_time: 18 min) – While throughput is stable, wait time and SLA breaches are material; this activity likely suffers from queue congestion due to upstream delays (e.g., Request_Documents).  
→ *Action: Decouple Initial_Assessment from Document_Request dependency via preliminary data pre-fill from customer portal; target 30% wait-time reduction.*  

*All other activities operate within acceptable ranges.* No other activities show statistically significant underperformance.
