**Question 2:** $\langle T1, T2, T7, T4, T5 \rangle$

**Explanation:** 
T1 creates tokens in both P1 and P2. While T3 consumes the token in P2, it is not required for T5 to fire; T5 only requires tokens in P2 and P5. The sequence $\langle T1, T2, T7, T4 \rangle$ places tokens in P2 and P5. Since P2 still contains its token from T1, T5 can fire immediately. T3 remains enabled but is not a prerequisite for T5, allowing T5 to execute first.
