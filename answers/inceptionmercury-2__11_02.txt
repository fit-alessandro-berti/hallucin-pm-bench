**Subject:** Q1‑2025 Claims‑Handling – Three Activities Needing Immediate Attention  

1. **Request Documents** – Avg wait = 150 min, rework = 22 %, SLA breach = 5.5 %. The extreme queue time and the highest repeat‑work rate indicate that claimants are repeatedly missing required items, forcing the team to restart the request cycle.  
   *Action:* Deploy an automated, AI‑guided document‑request portal that validates completeness before submission, cutting wait time and rework.

2. **Review Documents** – Avg proc = 25 min, stdev = 9 min, SLA breach = 12.4 %. Long, inconsistent processing and the largest breach percentage point to a lack of standardized review criteria.  
   *Action:* Introduce a structured checklist and a parallel‑review workflow, using data‑driven routing to balance workload and shrink both mean and variance of processing time.

3. **Initial Assessment** – Avg wait = 18 min, SLA breach = 3.2 %. The queue before assessment is the second‑longest, and the breach rate exceeds the process average, suggesting bottlenecks in triage.  
   *Action:* Implement a triage scoring model that prioritizes high‑risk claims, thereby reducing wait time and keeping assessments within SLA.

These targeted, data‑backed interventions should improve throughput, reduce variability, and bring SLA compliance back to target levels.
