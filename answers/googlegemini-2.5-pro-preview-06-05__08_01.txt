It is possible for transition T5 to fire before transition T3.

### Firing Sequence

A concrete firing sequence from the initial marking is: **T1, T2, T5**

### Justification

1.  **Initial Marking (M₀):**
    The net starts with one token in `p0`.
    *   `M₀ = (p0=1, p1=0, p2=0, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0)`

2.  **Fire T1:**
    T1 is the only enabled transition. It consumes the token from `p0` and places one token in `p1` and one in `p2`.
    *   `M₁ = (p0=0, p1=1, p2=1, p3=0, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0)`

3.  **Fire T2:**
    In marking `M₁`, both T2 (from `p1`) and T3 (from `p2`) are enabled. We can choose to fire T2. T2 consumes the token from `p1` and places a token in `p3`.
    *   `M₂ = (p0=0, p1=0, p2=1, p3=1, p4=0, p5=0, p6=0)`

4.  **Evaluate T5 Firing Condition:**
    At this point (marking `M₂`), let's check if T5 can fire. T5 is enabled if:
    *   Place `p3` has a token. (**True**, it received one from T2).
    *   Place `p4` has **zero** tokens (due to the inhibitor arc). (**True**, because T3 has not fired yet).

Since both conditions are met, T5 is enabled. We can fire it *before* firing T3, which is also enabled in this state. The proposed sequence is therefore valid.
