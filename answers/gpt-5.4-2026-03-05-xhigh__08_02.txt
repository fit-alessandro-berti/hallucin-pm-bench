**Q1. Yes.**  
`T5` can fire before `T3`.

**Q2. Minimal witness:**  
`[T1, T2, T7, T4, T5]`

Why: after `T1`, tokens are in `P1` and `P2`.  
Then `T2` and `T7` move the `P1`-branch token to `P4`, and `T4` moves it to `P5`.  
So after `T1, T2, T7, T4`, the marking contains `{P2, P5}`. That enables `T5`, while `T3` has still not fired.  

Hence `T5` does **not** depend on `T3`; it only needs the original `P2` token plus a `P5` token, which can be produced via the `P1 → P3 → P4 → P5` path.
