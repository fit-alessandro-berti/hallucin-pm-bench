**Yes**, it is possible for T5 to fire before T3.

**Concrete firing sequence:**
1. **T1** fires: \( p0 \to p1 + p2 \)  
   Marking: \( (0,1,1,0,0,0,0) \)
2. **T2** fires: \( p1 \to p3 \)  
   Marking: \( (0,0,1,1,0,0,0) \)  
   Now \( p3 = 1 \) and \( p4 = 0 \), so T5 is enabled (inhibitor arc from p4 is satisfied).
3. **T5** fires: \( p3 \to p6 \) (inhibited by p4)  
   Marking: \( (0,0,1,0,0,0,1) \)

At step 3, T5 fires while T3 has not yet fired (p2 still holds a token). This demonstrates that T5 can occur before T3.
