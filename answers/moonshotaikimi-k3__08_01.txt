**Yes.** A valid firing sequence is:

\[
M_0=(1,0,0,0,0,0,0)
\xrightarrow{T1}
(0,1,1,0,0,0,0)
\xrightarrow{T2}
(0,0,1,1,0,0,0)
\xrightarrow{T5}
(0,0,1,0,0,0,1)
\]

After \(T2\), place \(p3\) has one token while \(p4\) remains empty because \(T3\) has not fired. Thus \(T5\)'s inhibitor condition on \(p4\) is satisfied, allowing \(T5\) to fire **before \(T3\)**.
