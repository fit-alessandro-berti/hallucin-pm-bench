MEMORANDUM

TO: COO, Orion Credit
FROM: Senior Process-Performance Analyst
SUBJECT: Joint SLA-Breach and Waiting-Time Performance — Three Critical Activities

Based solely on the supplied eight-activity table, the three worst-performing processes are Legal Approval, Risk Review, and Credit Assessment, ranked jointly by SLA overrun magnitude and average waiting time.

Legal Approval records 900 s average throughput against a 600 s SLA, exceeding the target by 300 s; its average waiting time is 600 s across 465 cases.

Risk Review records 600 s throughput versus a 300 s SLA, also exceeding the target by 300 s, with a 480 s average wait across 480 cases.

Credit Assessment records 300 s throughput, 60 s above its 240 s SLA, with a 200 s average wait across 1,175 cases—the largest case volume among the breached trio.

Verify Identity also breaches (120 s vs. 90 s; +30 s; 80 s wait), but its combined breach and wait are smaller than those above.

Recommendations:
• Legal Approval: Launch parallel review tracks to cut the 600 s average waiting time—equal to the full SLA—directly reducing the 300 s throughput overrun observed in 465 cases.
• Risk Review: Implement prioritized scheduling to shrink the 480 s queue, which comprises 80 percent of the 600 s throughput, thereby recovering the 300 s SLA breach across 480 cases.
• Credit Assessment: Reallocate queue capacity from table activities with minimal waits—Customer Acceptance (15 s) and Draft Offer (30 s)—to reduce its 200 s bottleneck and close the 60 s gap over 1,175 cases.
