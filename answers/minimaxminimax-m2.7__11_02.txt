**Executive Memo – Q1‑2025 Claims Handling Performance**

**Three activities are driving most of the SLA and rework issues in Claims Handling:**

1. **Request Documents** – avg wait 150 min (the longest queue) and a 22 % rework rate, indicating repeated document requests. This backlog alone accounts for 5.5 % SLA breach.  
   *Recommendation:* Deploy an automated document‑checklist at claim registration and trigger immediate outreach to customers. Target a 60 % reduction in avg wait (≈90 min) to cut rework by at least half.

2. **Review Documents** – avg wait 30 min, avg processing 25 min, stdev 9 min, 5.3 % rework and the highest SLA breach (12.4 %). High variability and processing time signal a non‑standardized review.  
   *Recommendation:* Introduce a tiered review template with a fast‑track for low‑risk claims. Aim to lower avg processing to ≤15 min and reduce SLA breach to ≤5 %.

3. **Initial Assessment** – avg wait 18 min, processing 12 min, stdev 4 min, 1.8 % rework and 3.2 % SLA breach. The activity is a gate that, if delayed, cascades downstream.  
   *Recommendation:* Pre‑populate assessment fields using integrated external data (e.g., policy details) and shift to parallel data gathering. Target a 30 % cut in wait and a drop in SLA breach to ≤1 %.

These targeted, data‑driven changes should improve throughput, cut rework, and bring SLA compliance back to target.
