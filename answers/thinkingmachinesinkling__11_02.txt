**TO:** COO, Acme Insurance | **FROM:** Senior Performance Analyst | **RE:** Q1-2025 Claims Handling – Executive Brief

The Q1 dashboard reveals two unambiguous worst performers and one clear secondary bottleneck (with noted ambiguity on volume).

**1. Request_Documents** (190/day)  
*Why:* Throughput collapses from 270 upstream; wait time explodes to **150 min** (10× any other step); rework is **22.0%**; SLA breach is **5.5%**.  
*Action:* Launch a digital document-submission portal with mandatory field validation and automated reminders to cut the 150-min queue and directly reduce the 22% rework rate.

**2. Review_Documents** (185/day)  
*Why:* Longest processing time (**25 min**) and highest variability (**stdev 9 min**); rework **5.3%**; wait **30 min**; SLA breach peaks at **12.4%**.  
*Action:* Implement standardized review checklists and decision-support templates to stabilize the 25-min execution, reduce 9-min variability, and lower the 12.4% SLA breach.

**3. Initial_Assessment** (270/day)  
*Why:* Worst remaining quality profile—**18-min** wait, **12-min** processing, **4-min** stdev, **1.8%** rework, **3.2%** SLA breach. Downstream steps (Approve_Claim through Notify_Customer, **179–182/day**) post lower throughput but far stronger per-case metrics (wait ≤12 min, rework ≤0.8%, SLA ≤1.2%). Weighting throughput alone, Pay_Claim/Notify_Customer (**179**) tie for lowest; on combined wait, variability, rework, and SLA, Initial_Assessment is the clear third bottleneck.  
*Action:* Deploy rules-based automated triage with guided data entry to reduce the 18-min wait, 12-min processing, and 1.8% rework.

Fixing these three should restore flow and eliminate downstream starvation.
